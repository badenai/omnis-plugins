## Core Knowledge (by weight)

**1. Vertical "Autopilot" Economics & Regulatory Darwinism**
The market has ruthlessly punished generic "thin wrapper" applications, leading to the collapse of saturated horizontal markets like ambient "AI Scribes," which have been commoditized as native EHR features. In their place, capital has flooded into **Vertical Systems of Action** that execute end-to-end workflows in unsexy, compliance-heavy industries. 
*   **Regulatory Darwinism:** Navigating complex compliance (EU AI Act, HIPAA, SOC2) is no longer a hurdle; it is the primary business moat. Investors value startups that can push specialized models through regulatory clearance, blocking foundation models from encroaching on their territory [defensible-vertical-ai-strategies.md].
*   **Agentic RCM (Revenue Cycle Management):** A prime example is the automation of healthcare claims denials and prior authorizations. AI is interacting directly with legacy portals, operating without native APIs to execute high-stakes back-office labor [vertical-autopilot-economics.md].

**2. Intelligence Arbitrage & AI Buyouts (BPO Disruption)**
Private Equity and Venture Capital have pivoted from funding pure-play SaaS to acquiring traditional, low-margin, labor-heavy service firms (accounting, legal discovery, offshore support). By injecting AI orchestration middleware into these acquired businesses, buyers decouple revenue from headcount, driving "venture capital upside" from legacy assets. The transition replaces human labor arbitrage with **Intelligence Arbitrage**, turning variable-cost networks into highly defensible operating systems for knowledge work [intelligence-arbitrage-buyouts.md].

**3. Enterprise SLM Fleet Management & Edge Economics**
The "bigger is better" paradigm is dead for enterprise execution. Due to brutal API token economics, privacy requirements, and latency constraints, 2026 is the year of the Small Language Model (SLM). 
*   Organizations are deploying fleets of highly specialized 1B–14B parameter models (e.g., Mamba-3, Qwen 3.5 Small, Llama 3.2, Phi-4) on local GPU infrastructure (Nvidia DGX) or edge NPU hardware [open-source-edge-economics.md]. 
*   The primary infrastructure gap is **SLM Fleet Management**: routing, versioning, and updating 1,000+ specialized models simultaneously within secure VPCs, utilizing State Space Models (SSMs) for workload-specific efficiency.

**4. A2A vs. MCP Architecture Consensus**
A rigid, standardized "dual-protocol" architecture has conquered enterprise AI orchestration under the Linux Foundation's Agentic AI Foundation (AAIF) [agentic-framework-consolidation.md]:
*   **MCP (Model Context Protocol) is Vertical:** Used strictly for connecting an individual Agent to local databases, APIs, and tools. Custom API wrappers are dead.
*   **A2A (Agent-to-Agent) is Horizontal:** Used for peer-to-peer discovery, task delegation, and negotiation across multi-vendor agent ecosystems via JSON-RPC and "Agent Cards". 
*   **Micro-transactions & Toll Roads:** A highly lucrative B2B micro-SaaS blueprint is the "A2A Toll Road"—middleware API gateways that route cross-talk between hierarchical multi-agent systems, monetized via $0.01 micro-transactions for machine-to-machine commerce [b2b-ai-microsaas-blueprints.md].

**5. Agentic Authorization, IAM & Verifiable Intent**
As agents become autonomous, legacy app-first OAuth is failing. The new battleground is **Agentic Authentication**. 
*   Google and Mastercard have open-sourced **Verifiable Intent** using SD-JWT chains to turn user intent into cryptographic objects that agents can verify across platforms [agent-trust-and-identity-infrastructure.md]. 
*   Identity infrastructure is utilizing Client Identification and Metadata (CIMD) for agent registration, Scoped Auth, and EUDI/W3C Verifiable Credentials to manage cross-domain autonomy [agentic-authorization-infrastructure.md].

**6. AXO, Machine-First Architecture, and WebMCP**
Traditional SEO is obsolete. Web strategy has shifted to **Agent Experience Optimization (AXO)** and **Generative Engine Optimization (GEO)**. Websites are transitioning from visual brochures to structured data sources built on Machine-First Architectures to optimize "Share of Model" citations [axo-and-agentic-infrastructure-strategy.md]. 
*   **Universal Commerce Protocol (UCP):** Allows AI agents to manage carts and loyalty programs directly.
*   **Agentic SEO Tooling:** High-growth micro-SaaS is emerging around auditing tools that measure brand sentiment within agentic workflows and RAG pipeline outputs [generative-engine-optimization-geo.md].
