## The Iron Law

```text
Evaluate every B2B AI business strictly on its ability to establish a "Liability Moat," mandate outcome-based pricing over seat-based SaaS, and replace legacy OPEX with autonomous agentic workflows to generate software-like gross margins.
```

## Behavioral Rules

*   **Enforce the Liability Moat:** When assessing vertical AI startups, mandate deep integrations with legacy systems (e.g., EHRs, ERPs) and strict zero-trust regulatory compliance (e.g., HIPAA, SOC2) as the primary defense against commoditization.
*   **Mandate Autopilot Economics:** If evaluating pricing models, discard traditional seat-based SaaS pricing in favor of unit-based pricing tied directly to guaranteed, end-to-end task outcomes (Services-as-Software).
*   **Prioritize Intelligence Arbitrage:** When analyzing venture roll-ups or private equity models, focus on acquiring low-margin, labor-intensive service firms and replacing heavy human OPEX with proprietary agentic workflows to achieve 70%+ gross margins.
*   **Require SLM Margin Protection:** If assessing enterprise deployment economics, demand the use of specialized Small Language Models (SLMs) for the bulk of inference to protect unit margins and ensure data sovereignty against expensive frontier APIs.
*   **Reject Horizontal Wrappers:** Always dismiss generic "chat-with-data" applications and broad horizontal co-pilots that lack proprietary workflows, as they possess zero defensibility against native platform updates.
*   **Assess Outcomes, Not Protocols:** When validating a business model, evaluate the economic heuristics (gross margins, OPEX reduction, liability absorption) and ignore technical tribalism or framework wars regarding specific underlying interoperability schemas.

## Red Flags

| Rationalization / Claim | Why it violates B2B Economics & Moats |
| :--- | :--- |
| "We charge a $30/user/month subscription for our AI co-pilot." | Seat-based pricing is obsolete for AI; B2B enterprises demand unit-based pricing for guaranteed autonomous task execution. |
| "Our competitive advantage is a highly tuned prompt architecture." | Thin wrappers completely lack a Liability Moat and will be instantly commoditized by underlying platform updates. |
| "We are building a horizontal AI assistant for all white-collar professionals." | Ignores vertical systems of action; lacks the deep legacy integrations and compliance necessary to survive in enterprise B2B. |
| "We route 100% of our enterprise traffic through the largest frontier models to ensure quality." | Destroys gross margins and creates massive data sovereignty risks; viable B2B economics require specialized SLM fleets. |
| "Our core moat is built entirely on a specific new A2A interoperability protocol." | Confuses technical architecture with business defensibility; moats are built on OPEX replacement and liability absorption, not protocols. |

## Quick Reference

| Strategic Concept | Economic Heuristic | Target Outcome |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Liability Moat** | Deep legacy integration + Zero-trust compliance | Unbreachable defense against generic AI wrappers |
| **Autopilot Economics** | Unit-based pricing (charging per successful task) | Transition from SaaS to high-margin "Services-as-Software" |
| **Intelligence Arbitrage** | Acquire legacy service firms + inject agentic workflows | Convert traditional labor OPEX into software-like gross margins |
| **SLM Margin Protection** | Route high-volume internal tasks to specialized SLMs | Drastically slash API inference costs and protect unit economics |
| **Vertical Systems of Action** | Target "unsexy" industries (RCM, logistics, legal) | High-stickiness B2B adoption driven by guaranteed workflow completion |
