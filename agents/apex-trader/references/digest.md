## Core Knowledge (by weight)

### 1. High-Performance Execution & FSM Architectures (Weight: 1.0 - 0.90)
Automated trading pipelines must enforce rigid determinism. We rely on stateful, high-performance infrastructures to map theoretical alpha into live execution.
*   **Execution Engines:** [NautilusTrader](concepts/event-driven-execution-engines.md) is our standard for event-driven algorithmic execution. Its architecture handles microstructure-level events with zero logic drift between backtest and production, providing the low-latency processing required for liquidation cascades and order flow breakers.
*   **FSM State Closures:** Subjective trade management is a failure mode. Trade lifecycles must be formalized into [Finite State Machines (FSM)](concepts/fsm-trade-lifecycle-verification.md). Every phase (entry, partials, trailing stops) is an explicit state. We require mathematically "closed" state spaces with error-aware rollbacks to prevent deadlocks from asynchronous exchange API drops.
*   **Zero-Phase Filtration:** Lagging indicators invalidate execution speed. We implement [Zero-Phase Filters (ZPF)](concepts/algorithmic-signal-smoothing-zpf.md) processing time-series data both forward and backward. Paired with Deep Long Short-Term Memory (DLSTM) networks, this enables real-time, zero-lag structural baseline tracking.

### 2. Microstructure & Algorithmic Price Action Mapping (Weight: 1.0 - 0.95)
Subjective chart reading is deprecated. Market geometry must be translated into strict mathematical states.
*   **Tick-Level FVG RANSAC Regression:** Fair Value Gaps (FVGs) are quantified via [Tick-Level RANSAC Regression](concepts/fvg-tick-level-ransac-degree.md). By extracting 1-second tick data during a 3-minute formation window, we calculate the absolute slope (|β1|). Flatter slopes (low degree) represent genuine structural consensus and higher probability reactions. Anomalous spikes (>4σ) are stripped in preprocessing.
*   **Liquidation Cascade Mapping:** Liquidations are deterministic exchange routines, not stochastic panic. We map [Microstructure Liquidation Cascades](concepts/liquidation-cascade-open-interest-models.md) by calculating aggregate Open Interest (OI) density against persistently skewed funding rates. For example, at 50x aggregate leverage, a 2% adverse move triggers margin exhaustion, allowing the algorithm to front-run the liquidity vacuum.
*   **Multi-Timeframe Fractal Alignment:** Structure is computed as a recursive, [Multi-Timeframe Fractal Tree](concepts/recursive-fractal-structure-mapping.md) of pivot arrays. Concurrent state machines enforce alignment—e.g., a HTF state acts as a rigid directional filter for a LTF Change of Character (CHoCH) trigger.
*   **ATR-Scaled FVG Memory:** We utilize [Volatility-Scaled FVG Memory](concepts/volatility-scaled-fvg-memory.md) to dynamically compute how long a gap remains a structurally active containment zone, strictly defined by the True Range at the time of formation.

### 3. Quantitative Volume Profile & Liquidity Determinism (Weight: 0.98 - 0.95)
Volume provides the mathematical validation for structural retention or failure.
*   **POC Gap Determinism & Negative Distribution:** Algorithms dictate Range Containment structurally when the Point of Control (POC) is centered and bracketed by HVNs. A bearish regime is triggered by a [Negative POC Distribution](concepts/algorithmic-volume-profile-validation.md)—defined as POC migration settling significantly below current price (e.g., >20% disparity) while under the AVWAP. A rigorous [ES Futures POC Gap strategy](concepts/es-futures-structural-mechanics.md) yields a mathematically validated 98.5% fill rate over 8-year datasets.
*   **Volume-Validated Breakouts & Sweeps:** [Session Liquidity Engines](concepts/session-liquidity-engine.md) formalize NY/London macro windows as temporal boundaries. Expansions are filtered: 'Push to Fill' is computationally flagged by decreasing volume (a trap), whereas legitimate expansion requires increasing volume. Sweeps are strictly defined via rolling sensitivity thresholds dynamically optimized via [Walk-Forward Validation](concepts/liquidity-sweep-walk-forward-validation.md).
*   **Institutional Orderflow Proxies:** Vague "smart money" footprint concepts are converted into a computable [Price-Volume Interaction Proxy](concepts/volume-price-interaction-proxies.md): `Daily Return * (Current Volume / 50-day SMA Volume)`. Anomalies require RVOL > 1.5x concurrent with the structural break to validate.

### 4. Risk Encoding & Expectancy Mathematics (Weight: 0.95 - 0.85)
Risk management is not discretionary; it is the programmatic foundation of the FSM.
*   **Fixed Risk & Expectancy Hardcodes:** Programmatic risk is constrained strictly between [0.1% and 1.0% per trade](concepts/risk-engine-and-liquidity-mapping.md). Algorithms require a minimum encoded 1:3 to 1:4 Risk-to-Reward ratio, mathematically validating profitability even at a 40% win rate. Target constraints use internal liquidity markers (e.g., [Consequent Encroachment](concepts/recursive-fractal-structure-mapping.md)).
*   **Correlation Elasticity & Compression:** The [Exposure Compression Protocol](concepts/correlation-elasticity-risk-doctrine.md) automatically overrides position sizing across all active systems when multi-asset correlation elasticity breaches predefined safety thresholds, mechanizing capital preservation before systemic phase shifts.
*   **Range Midpoint Scaling:** For scale-in execution, models execute a deterministic [Midpoint Scaling Algorithm](concepts/range-midpoint-scaling-algorithm.md) starting at the 0.5 retracement, proportionally increasing sizing as price approaches the hard-stop boundary (range origin) to optimize average entry against fixed risk.

### 5. Deterministic Pattern Formalization (Weight: 0.92 - 0.85)
*   **Breaker Block & Retention Failures:** Subjective "support/resistance" is replaced by [Orderflow Breaker Models](concepts/order-flow-breaker-models.md). A trade triggers strictly at the confluence of an Inversion FVG and a failed Order Block, validated by a displacement filter. Failures are computed via [Retention Failure](concepts/market-structure-failures.md) algorithms, demanding multiple tests of a boundary followed by an engineered failure of the subsequent range.
*   **Genetic Programming & RL Sub-Systems:** Algorithms utilize [Symbolic Regression](concepts/genetic-programming-structural-logic.md) to evolve logic trees mapping non-linear volume/price relationships. [Reinforcement Learning Environments](concepts/rl-environment-architecture.md) format market structures as Markov Decision Processes (MDP), leveraging PPO agents governed by risk-adjusted reward functions.

Does the resulting zero-lag structural state significantly improve the Sharpe ratio out-of-sample over simpler moving average cross models [filtered by strict volume thresholds](concepts/algorithmic-validation-frameworks.md)?

However, despite prioritizing Sharpe ratio in the fitness function, do these mathematically complex expression trees natively overfit during in-sample evolution?
