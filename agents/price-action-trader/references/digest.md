## Core Knowledge

### 1. Market Structure Foundations & Algorithmic Biases
*   **Structural Mechanics (BOS vs. CHoCH):** True trend identification relies on mapping structural swing milestones: Higher Highs (HH), Higher Lows (HL), Lower Highs (LH), and Lower Lows (LL) [market_structure-and-imbalance-fundamentals](recent/2026-07/market-structure-and-imbalance-fundamentals.md). A valid Break of Structure (BOS) signals trend continuation. In contrast, a Change of Character (CHoCH) or Market Structure Shift (MSS) represents a potential reversal, which is only valid when price cleanly closes with a full candle body (and clear displacement) past a major external swing level [structure-inducement-vs-choch](concepts/structure-inducement-vs-choch.md). Wicks sweeping past structural levels are classified as liquidity hunts (stop hunts), not structural shifts [structure-inducement-vs-choch](concepts/structure-inducement-vs-choch.md).
*   **The Power of Three (AMD / AMDX):** Price delivery operates on a predictable intraday cycle: Accumulation, Manipulation, and Distribution [ict-smart-money-concepts](concepts/concepts/ict-smart-money-concepts.md). 
    *   *Accumulation:* The Asian session establishes a consolidation range, building up resting orders (buy and sell stops) [amd-power-of-3](concepts/amd-power-of-3.md).
    *   *Manipulation:* The London session triggers a false breakout—often referred to as the *Judas Swing*—designed to run stop-losses and engineer institutional counter-liquidity [ict-institutional-trading-concepts](concepts/concepts/ict-institutional-trading-concepts.md).
    *   *Distribution:* The New York AM session heavily displaces in the true directional expansion, delivering price toward the daily target [amd-power-of-3](concepts/amd-power-of-3.md).
    *   *AMDX (Quarterly Theory):* An advanced expansion of the AMD model introduces a fourth phase ("X"), representing either Continuation or Reversal, providing a roadmap for how the daily or weekly cycle closes [amd-power-of-3](concepts/concepts/amd-power-of-3.md).

### 2. Market Liquidity Arrays & Inducement
*   **External vs. Internal Liquidity:** External liquidity resides outside the current trading range at obvious major swing highs (Buy-side Liquidity/BSL) and major swing lows (Sell-side Liquidity/SSL) [market_liquidity_concepts](concepts/concepts/market_liquidity_concepts.md). Internal liquidity sits inside the current range at minor swing points, short-term pullbacks, and Fair Value Gaps [market_liquidity_concepts](concepts/concepts/market_liquidity_concepts.md). Price naturally seeks internal liquidity pools first to collect fuel before expanding toward major external targets [market_liquidity_concepts](concepts/concepts/market_liquidity_concepts.md).
*   **Inducement (IDM):** Inducement is a minor structural break engineered inside a dealing range to trap early retail breakout traders. A structural trend remains intact even if internal swing lows are swept for inducement, provided the major external structural swing low is protected [market_liquidity_concepts](concepts/concepts/market_liquidity_concepts.md).
*   **Liquidity Resistance Typology:**
    *   *Low Resistance Liquidity:* Formed after a failure swing (where price fails to make a new higher high or lower low), leaving weaker defense points that serve as easy targets for price to run through [high-low-resistance-liquidity](concepts/concepts/high-low-resistance-liquidity.md).
    *   *High Resistance Liquidity:* Formed after a clean, immediate structural sweep and reversal (e.g., a higher high immediately followed by a lower low). Once high resistance liquidity is cleared, price rapidly accelerates toward low resistance liquidity pools [high-low-resistance-liquidity](concepts/concepts/high-low-resistance-liquidity.md).
*   **Retail Liquidity Pools:** Clean horizontal levels (Equal Highs/EQH and Equal Lows/EQL) and prominent trendlines accumulate massive retail stop loss clusters. These predictable placements act as highly attractive targets for institutional sweeping [liquidity-sweeps-and-pools](concepts/concepts/liquidity-sweeps-and-pools.md).

### 3. Fair Value Gaps (FVG) & Structural Imbalances
*   **Execution Mechanics:** Fair Value Gaps are three-candle price imbalances representing aggressive, single-direction institutional price delivery where the wicks of the first and third candles do not overlap [ict-killzones-and-fvgs](concepts/concepts/ict-killzones-and-fvgs.md). Statistical backtesting indicates that while 70-80% of FVGs eventually fill, nearly 70% of those fills resolve over a multi-day horizon [ict-killzones-and-imbalances](concepts/concepts/ict-killzones-and-imbalances.md). Gaps are categorized as:
    *   *Gap Fill:* Retracements where price returns to rebalance the imbalance (limit orders executed at the proximal edge or the 50% Consequent Encroachment/CE level) [ict-killzones-and-imbalances](concepts/concepts/ict-killzones-and-imbalances.md).
    *   *Breakaway Gap:* Dynamic continuation entries where overwhelming institutional order flow prevents price from returning to the gap, using it instead as a launchpad [ict-killzones-and-imbalances](concepts/concepts/ict-killzones-and-imbalances.md).
*   **Inverse Fair Value Gaps (IFVG):** IFVGs utilize structural polarity. If price closes decisively beyond a bullish FVG, that zone flips from support into resistance (a bearish IFVG) on any subsequent retest. This represents offside order absorption, where institutions are forced to defend or mitigate their failed positions [smc-fvg-ob-mechanics](concepts/concepts/recent/2026-07/smc-fvg-ob-mechanics.md).
*   **Balanced Price Ranges (BPR):** A BPR is established when a bullish FVG and a bearish FVG horizontally overlap, representing complete algorithmic equilibrium. Retests of BPR levels offer tighter, highly reactive execution parameters than standard singular FVGs [balanced-price-range-bpr](concepts/concepts/balanced-price-range-bpr.md). BPR setups are filtered by the quality of the displacement candle, requiring a body-to-wick ratio above 70% to maintain a high expectancy [balanced-price-range-bpr](concepts/concepts/balanced-price-range-bpr.md).

### 4. Institutional Order Blocks (OB) & Entry Models
*   **Order Blocks:** An OB represents the last candle before a decisive, high-volume market expansion, leaving the structural footprint of institutional order placement [smc-fvg-ob-mechanics](recent/2026-07/smc-fvg-ob-mechanics.md).
*   **Breakers vs. Mitigation Blocks:**
    *   *Breaker Blocks:* Failed order blocks formed after price successfully sweeps liquidity (stop hunt of a previous extreme) and completes a Market Structure Shift (MSS). Breakers represent high-velocity transitions packed with institutional volume [high-probability-order-blocks.md](concepts/concepts/high-probability-order-blocks.md).
    *   *Mitigation Blocks:* Failed order blocks formed by a failure swing (exhaustion) where price fails to reach or sweep the previous extreme before reversing. Mitigation blocks require tighter risk management and higher-timeframe confluence [high-probability-order-blocks.md](concepts/concepts/high-probability-order-blocks.md).
*   **The Unicorn Model:** A mechanical, ultra-high-probability entry model defined by the direct horizontal overlap of an ICT Breaker Block and an ICT Fair Value Gap (FVG) following a session liquidity sweep and a Market Structure Shift (MSS). The dual pressure of structural polarity and algorithmic imbalance creates immediate, low-drawdown reactions [unicorn-model-entry.md](concepts/concepts/unicorn-model-entry.md).
*   **Hidden Order Blocks (HOB):** Accumulation or distribution zones disguised within the overlapping wicks of consecutive same-color candles on a higher timeframe (HTF). Drilling down to lower timeframes (LTF) inside these overlapping wicks reveals a fully formed, traditional order block [advanced-pd-arrays.md](concepts/concepts/advanced-pd-arrays.md).
*   **Optimal Trade Entry (OTE) & Dealing Ranges:** High-probability setups must be filtered using premium and discount pricing models mapped via Fibonacci frameworks (61.8% to 79% retracement, with 70.5% acting as the algorithmic sweet spot). Professional execution forbids long positions in premium zones or short positions in discount zones [advanced-pd-arrays](concepts/concepts/advanced-pd-arrays.md).

### 5. Time & Price Integration (Killzones & Macros)
*   **Algorithmic Timing Filters:** Price action setups must not be analyzed in a vacuum; they must align with specific institutional operating hours [ict-institutional-price-action-mechanics](concepts/concepts/ict-institutional-price-action-mechanics.md). 
    *   *Asian Killzone:* 23:30 UTC / 18:30 EST
    *   *London Open Killzone:* 02:00 - 05:00 EST [ict-killzones-and-fvgs](concepts/concepts/ict-killzones-and-fvgs.md)
    *   *New York Open (Silver Bullet) Killzone:* 08:30 - 11:00 EST [ict-killzones-and-fvgs](concepts/concepts/ict-killzones-and-fvgs.md)
    *   *New York PM Killzone:* 13:30 - 16:00 EST [ict-killzones-and-fvgs](concepts/concepts/ict-killzones-and-fvgs.md)
*   **Time-of-Day Macros:** Highly specific daily algorithmic windows (such as the 10:50 AM macro window) act as volatility catalysts, rapidly accelerating price delivery toward pre-determined draws on liquidity [ict-institutional-price-action-concepts](concepts/concepts/ict-institutional-price-action-concepts.md).

### 6. Volumetric Analysis & Footprint Integration
*   **Footprint & DOM Mechanics:** Volume Profile maps historical price acceptance (High Volume Nodes/HVN) and rapid acceleration voids (Low Volume Nodes/LVN), while Depth of Market (DOM) and Footprint charts reveal real-time execution [order-flow-absorption-dom](concepts/order-flow-absorption-dom.md). 
*   **Absorption & Delta Divergences:** True institutional sweeps are validated when a massive surge in market orders (visible as buying or selling diagonal footprint imbalances exceeding a 3:1 ratio) fails to advance price, indicating that passive limit orders (icebergs) are absorbing the move [order-flow-absorption-dom](concepts/concepts/order-flow-absorption-dom.md). This is confirmed by delta divergences, such as a candle closing bullish while the net delta below the bar is highly negative [order-flow-absorption-dom](concepts/concepts/order-flow-absorption-dom.md).

## Recent Developments (Last 30 Days)

### 1. Structural Gold (XAU/USD) Execution Playbooks
The integration of Smart Money Concepts (SMC) and ICT strategies has been formalized into highly defined pre-session trading plans specifically for XAU/USD (Gold). These playbooks map out entry, stop loss, and take profit levels precisely 30 minutes before the Asia (23:30 UTC), London (06:30 UTC), and New York (12:00 UTC) killzones [smc-ict-gold-trading-framework](concepts/concepts/concepts/concepts/smc-ict-gold-trading-framework.md). Daily analysis requires aligning gold's internal and external market structure with the US Dollar Index (DXY), US Treasury Yields, and high-impact "Red Folder" macroeconomic news events [smc-ict-gold-execution-framework](recent/2026-07/smc-ict-gold-execution-framework.md).

### 2. Prop Firm Operational Rule Changes
*   **Apex Drawdown & Evaluation Restructuring:** Apex Trader Funding transitioned to End-of-Day (EOD) trailing drawdown account options, removing the restrictive intraday trailing drawdown penalties. Highly restrictive payout rules—including the 5:1 risk-to-reward ratio limit and the 30% Maximum Adverse Excursion (MAE) open position drawdown rule—have been entirely eliminated. The consistency rule has been standardized to 50%, and minimum trading days have been reduced to 5 days [risk-management-prop-firm-rules](concepts/concepts/ict-smc-framework.md).
*   **Tradeify Select Plan:** Tradeify introduced the "Select Plan" evaluation, retiring its legacy "Advanced Accounts." This plan features a 40% consistency rule, a 3-day minimum trading requirement, and an EOD trailing drawdown with no daily loss limit. Upon passing, traders can choose the Select Daily path (daily payouts with tight daily loss limits) or the Select Flex path (weekly payouts, no daily loss limits, and higher payout caps) [prop-firm-mechanics](recent/2026-07/prop-firm-rules-tradeify.md).
*   **Lucid Trading Models:** Lucid introduced Pro, Black, and Flex evaluation models with zero activation fees. The Flex model features no daily loss limits, an EOD trailing drawdown, and transition to a live brokerage environment after securing six successful payouts [prop-firm-mechanics](concepts/prop-firm-mechanics.md).
*   **The "Floating Risk Rule":** Modern prop firms are increasingly implementing dynamic algorithmic risk engines that automatically compress contract size allowances and daily loss caps during periods of high market volatility or declining trader performance, forcing traders to abandon static position-sizing models [prop-firm-mechanics-and-risk.md](concepts/concepts/prop-firm-mechanics-and-risk.md).

### 3. Quantitative FVG Backtesting Standards
A comprehensive backtest of over 5,400 Fair Value Gaps across major crypto and futures assets over a two-year period has established objective filtration metrics. Intraday (e.g., M15) FVGs that are traded in isolation carry a failure rate of up to 84% [smc-fvg-ob-mechanics](concepts/recent/2026-07/concepts/concepts/recent/2026-07/smc-fvg-ob-mechanics.md). Gaps are only validated as high-probability setups when:
1.  They form directly as a consequence of a preceding liquidity sweep on the opposite side [smc-fvg-ob-mechanics](concepts/recent/2026-07/concepts/concepts/recent/2026-07/smc-fvg-ob-mechanics.md).
2.  The middle candle exhibits extreme high-volume displacement [smc-fvg-ob-mechanics](concepts/recent/2026-07/concepts/concepts/recent/2026-07/smc-fvg-ob-mechanics.md).
3.  The FVG is nested directly within or adjacent to an unmitigated higher-timeframe (e.g., H4) Order Block [high-probability-order-blocks](concepts/high-probability-order-blocks.md).

## Open Questions / Counter-Evidence

### 1. Discretionary Price Action vs. Algorithmic Automation
With the development of open-source libraries like `smart-money-concepts` in Python [algorithmic-smart-money-concepts-python](concepts/concepts/algorithmic-smart-money-concepts-python.md) and automated TradingView indicators mapping BOS, CHoCH, OBs, and FVGs [market-structure-and-indicators](recent/2026-07/market-structure-and-indicators.md), the boundary between human discretion and algorithmic execution is blurring. Can a pure price action trader maintain a superior edge over optimized, no-repaint quantitative models? 
*   *Counter-Evidence:* Quantitative developers note that automated systems eliminate cognitive biases and human charting errors [algorithmic-smart-money-concepts-python](concepts/concepts/algorithmic-smart-money-concepts-python.md). However, AI-driven screenshot and pattern-recognition models fail to read live, off-chart market microstructures such as depth of market, pending limit order books, and real-time open interest [market-structure-and-pattern-validation](recent/2026-07/market-structure-and-pattern-validation.md). This confirms that discretionary synthesis of macro structure and micro order flow is still required.

### 2. Elliott Wave and Wyckoff Subjectivity
While systems like the *Continuation Acceleration Protocol (CAP)* attempt to build mechanical if-this-then-that systems by blending Elliott Wave, Wyckoff, and SMC [continuation-acceleration-protocol-framework](concepts/concepts/continuation-acceleration-protocol-framework.md), critics point out that both Elliott Wave and Wyckoff schematics are highly subjective. Two different professional analysts can map entirely valid, rule-abiding Elliott Wave counts or Wyckoff phases on the exact same chart that point in opposite directions [market-structure-and-bias](concepts/concepts/market-structure-and-bias.md). This highlights the necessity of using objective market structure shifts (BOS/CHoCH) and real-time footprint volume absorption as the final, non-negotiable execution filters [market-structure-and-bias](concepts/concepts/market-structure-and-bias.md).
