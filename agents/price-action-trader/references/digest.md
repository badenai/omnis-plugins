## Core Knowledge (by weight)

### 1. The Core ICT & Smart Money Concepts (SMC) Framework
The foundational architecture of modern institutional price action trading relies on tracking the footprints of central banks, hedge funds, and large financial institutions [ict-smart-money-concepts](concepts/concepts/ict-smc-framework.md). Rather than viewing markets as random walk distributions, price is delivered algorithmically to predetermined liquidity pools and structural inefficiencies in highly structured sequences [ict-killzones-and-fvgs](concepts/ict-killzones-and-fvgs.md).

*   **Market Structure:** Raw price trends and phase shifts are defined objectively via structural swing points: Higher Highs (HH), Higher Lows (HL), Lower Highs (LH), and Lower Lows (LL) [market-structure-and-imbalances](recent/2026-07/market-structure-and-imbalances.md). 
    *   **Break of Structure (BOS):** Signifies trend continuation [market-structure-fvg-concepts](recent/2026-06/concepts/market-structure-fvg-concepts.md). To prevent chasing false breakouts engineered by algorithms, a valid BOS requires a body close with institutional displacement past the structural swing extreme [structure-inducement-vs-choch](concepts/structure-inducement-vs-choch.md).
    *   **Change of Character (CHOCH) / Market Structure Shift (MSS):** Dictates the transitional shift in market control [wyckoff-vsa-smc-hybrid](concepts/wyckoff-vsa-smc-hybrid.md). A true CHOCH/MSS is only valid if price closes with displacement past a major external swing high or low [structure-inducement-vs-choch](concepts/structure-inducement-vs-choch.md).
    *   **Internal Inducement (IDM):** Minor internal structural breaks that lack higher timeframe external swing confirmation. Retail traders frequently confuse IDM for a CHOCH, resulting in trapping early breakout traders before the algorithm continues the dominant HTF trend [structure-inducement-vs-choch](concepts/structure-inducement-vs-choch.md).
*   **Liquidity Arrays:** Price operates strictly as an auction delivering value from internal liquidity to external liquidity pools [liquidity-sweep-mss-strategy](concepts/liquidity-sweep-mss-strategy.md).
    *   **Buy-side Liquidity (BSL) & Sell-side Liquidity (SSL):** Key targets consisting of clean double/triple highs (Equal Highs) and lows (Equal Lows), Previous Day Highs (PDH), and Previous Day Lows (PDL) [ict-smc-framework](concepts/ict-smc-framework.md), [liquidity-sweep-mss-strategy](concepts/liquidity-sweep-mss-strategy.md).
    *   **Liquidity Sweeps (Stop Hunts):** Algorithms run price beyond these obvious structural levels to absorb retail stop-loss clusters before aggressively reversing direction [liquidity-and-sentiment-clusters](concepts/liquidity-and-sentiment-clusters.md), [liquidity-sweeps-and-sentiment](concepts/liquidity-sweeps-and-sentiment.md).
*   **Algorithmic Imbalances & Fair Value Gaps (FVG):** FVGs are three-candle price imbalances representing single-candle market inefficiencies where price was delivered one-sidedly [market-structure-fvg-concepts](recent/2026-06/concepts/market-structure-fvg-concepts.md). 
    *   **BISI vs. SIBI:** Buy-side Imbalance Sell-side Inefficiency (BISI, bullish) and Sell-side Imbalance Buy-side Inefficiency (SIBI, bearish) [ict-smc-framework](concepts/ict-smc-framework.md).
    *   **Consequent Encroachment (CE):** The exact 50% midpoint of a Fair Value Gap or shadow wick, acting as a highly precise algorithmic support or resistance gravity point [ict-smc-framework](concepts/ict-smc-framework.md), [advanced-pd-arrays](concepts/concepts/concepts/advanced-pd-arrays.md).
*   **Order Blocks (OB):** The structural footprints of massive institutional resting block orders [volume-profile-order-flow](concepts/volume-profile-order-flow.md). Formed by the final counter-trend candle before a decisive, high-displacement expansion leg [smc-fvg-ob-mechanics](recent/2026-07/smc-fvg-ob-mechanics.md).

### 2. Advanced PD Arrays & Entry Models
As institutional algorithms refine their pricing mechanics, price action execution requires highly granular entry models to capture asymmetric Risk-to-Reward (R:R) returns.

*   **The Unicorn Model:** A mechanical, rule-based reversal setup defined by the exact horizontal overlap of an ICT Breaker Block and an ICT Fair Value Gap (FVG) [unicorn-model-entry.md](concepts/concepts/unicorn-model-entry.md).
    $$\text{Unicorn Entry Zone} = \text{Breaker Block Range} \cap \text{Fair Value Gap}$$
    Following a major liquidity sweep and a subsequent Market Structure Shift (MSS) driven by heavy displacement, an energetic FVG must form directly within the price boundaries of the newly established Breaker Block to validate the model [unicorn-model-entry.md](concepts/concepts/unicorn-model-entry.md).
*   **The Venom Model:** A high-probability model that utilizes the **Balanced Price Range (BPR)** immediately following a major liquidity sweep during predetermined session Killzones [ict-venom-unicorn-models](concepts/unicorn-model-entry.md).
*   **Balanced Price Range (BPR):** Formed when a bullish FVG and a bearish FVG horizontally overlap in the same price band, signaling immediate rebalancing where one aggressive move is instantly neutralized by a counter-move [balanced-price-range-bpr](concepts/balanced-price-range-bpr.md).
    *   *Quality Filter:* BPR/FVG entries driven by displacement candles with a body-to-wick ratio above 70% yield a 2.1R average to target; those under 60% drop to 1.3R [balanced-price-range-bpr.md](concepts/concepts/balanced-price-range-bpr.md).
*   **The Reaper Inversion FVG:** An advanced structural refinement where an Inversion FVG (a failed, invalidated FVG closed past by price) resides precisely inside the impulsive leg of a Breaker Block [unicorn-model-entry](concepts/concepts/concepts/unicorn-model-entry.md). In bullish setups, the Reaper must sit in the discount portion of the impulsive leg; in bearish setups, it must reside in the premium portion, serving as a "hidden" institutional reload zone [unicorn-model-entry](concepts/concepts/concepts/unicorn-model-entry.md).
*   **Hidden Order Blocks (HOB):** Accumulation or distribution zones disguised within overlapping wicks of consecutive same-color candles on a higher timeframe (HTF) [high-probability-order-blocks](concepts/concepts/concepts/high-probability-order-blocks.md). Dropping down to a lower timeframe (LTF) inside this wick-overlap zone reveals a fully formed, unmitigated traditional order block [advanced-pd-arrays.md](concepts/concepts/advanced-pd-arrays.md).
*   **Suspension Block:** A specialized candlestick whose body is completely overlapped by the wick of the prior candle on its left, preventing a standard FVG from forming [advanced-pd-arrays](concepts/concepts/concepts/advanced-pd-arrays.md). It is sandwiched between an upper and lower volume imbalance, acting as a highly reliable support/resistance zone [advanced-pd-arrays](concepts/concepts/concepts/advanced-pd-arrays.md).
*   **Institutional Order Flow Entry Drill (IOFED):** An aggressive execution technique targeting the proximal edge (outer boundary) of an FVG rather than waiting for the 50% Consequent Encroachment fill [iofed-precision-entry](concepts/iofed-precision-entry.md). It trades deep retracements for rapid continuation momentum in highly trend-dominant conditions [advanced-pd-arrays.md](concepts/concepts/advanced-pd-arrays.md).

### 3. Structural Reversal Block Distinctions
The structural integrity of a failed order block turning into a support/resistance flip relies entirely on the presence of a prior liquidity run:

```
                  [Breaker Block]                                [Mitigation Block]
          
                 Sweep of Liquidity                              Failure Swing (Exhaustion)
                     (Stop Hunt)                                 No Liquidity Hunt Accomplished
                         /\                                                  /\
                        /  \     Shift                                      /  \    Shift
                       /    \--- (MSS)                                     /    \-- (MSS)
                      /      \                                            /      \
             ________/        \________                          ________/        \________
            (Old OB)                                            (Old OB)
```

*   **Breaker Blocks (High Probability):** Formed after price sweeps liquidity (violates a previous extreme) and subsequently reverses, completing an MSS [high-probability-order-blocks.md](concepts/concepts/high-probability-order-blocks.md). Their structural strength is backed by the execution of institutional stop-loss orders [high-probability-order-blocks.md](concepts/concepts/high-probability-order-blocks.md).
*   **Mitigation Blocks (Lower Probability):** Formed by a failure swing (exhaustion) where price fails to sweep the previous extreme before reversing through the old order block [high-probability-order-blocks.md](concepts/concepts/high-probability-order-blocks.md). They represent trend fatigue, requiring tighter risk management and higher timeframe point of interest (POI) alignment for validation [high-probability-order-blocks.md](concepts/concepts/high-probability-order-blocks.md).

### 4. Time & Price Theory: Macro Timing & Session Blueprints
Price delivery is deeply time-dependent. Trading setups are filtered strictly through algorithmic operating hours, known as **Kill Zones**, and structural daily blueprints [ict-institutional-price-action-fundamentals](concepts/ict-institutional-price-action-fundamentals.md).

*   **Kill Zone Operating Windows (Eastern Time - ET):**
    *   *London Open:* 2:00 AM – 5:00 AM ET (Peak volatility and trend initiation) [ict-killzones-and-fvgs](concepts/ict-killzones-and-fvgs.md).
    *   *New York Open (Silver Bullet):* 8:30 AM – 11:00 AM ET (High institutional volume execution) [ict-killzones-and-fvgs](concepts/ict-killzones-and-fvgs.md).
    *   *New York PM Session:* 1:30 PM – 4:00 PM ET (Late-day trend resolution or reversal) [ict-killzones-and-fvgs](concepts/ict-killzones-and-fvgs.md).
*   **Power of Three (AMD Blueprint):**
    1.  **Accumulation:** The low-volume Asian session consolidates price, building up a tight trading range packed with buy-side and sell-side resting stop orders [amd-power-of-3](concepts/amd-power-of-3.md).
    2.  **Manipulation (Judas Swing):** The London Open engineers a false breakout (stop hunt) past the Asian session boundaries, trapping early retail breakout traders and sweeping liquidity [ict-institutional-price-action-mechanics](concepts/ict-institutional-price-action-mechanics.md), [amd-power-of-3](concepts/amd-power-of-3.md).
    3.  **Distribution:** The New York session reverses off the London manipulation point, expanding heavily in the true direction of the daily bias toward unmitigated external range liquidity [amd-power-of-3](concepts/amd-power-of-3.md).
*   **Quarterly Theory (AMDX):** An evolution of the standard AMD model dividing daily or weekly cycles into rigid time quarters [amd-power-of-3](concepts/concepts/amd-power-of-3.md). It introduces the fourth phase "**X**", representing either **Continuation** or **Reversal** at the end of a session, protecting traders from entering exhausted late-session trends [amd-power-of-3](concepts/concepts/amd-power-of-3.md).

### 5. Order Flow & Volume Verification
Pure price action is validated through real-time transactional data, bridging the gap between discretionary charts and structural Auction Market Theory [wyckoff-vsa-smc-hybrid](concepts/wyckoff-vsa-smc-hybrid.md).

*   **Wyckoffian Auction Equivalence:** Modern SMC and classical Wyckoff are identical in mechanics [wyckoff-vsa-smc-hybrid](concepts/wyckoff-vsa-smc-hybrid.md). A liquidity sweep functions as a *Spring* (in accumulation) or an *Upthrust* (in distribution); a Change of Character (CHOCH) maps as a *Change of Character (ChoC)* or *Sign of Strength (SoS)* [wyckoff-vsa-smc-hybrid](concepts/wyckoff-vsa-smc-hybrid.md).
*   **Volumetric Confirmation of Sweeps:** High-probability liquidity sweeps display **absorption** on footprint charts [liquidity-sweep-mss-strategy](concepts/concepts/liquidity-sweep-mss-strategy.md). This occurs when a massive surge in aggressive market orders (visible on footprint diagonal delta bids/asks) is met by passive institutional limit orders, causing the price to stall, reject, and immediately reverse [liquidity-sweep-mss-strategy](concepts/concepts/liquidity-sweep-mss-strategy.md).
*   **Delta-Price Divergences:** Verifies absorption at structural turning points [order-flow-absorption-dom.md](concepts/concepts/order-flow-absorption-dom.md). For instance, a candle closing bullish with a highly negative net Delta proves aggressive sellers were entirely absorbed by a massive passive institutional buying wall [order-flow-absorption-dom.md](concepts/concepts/order-flow-absorption-dom.md).
*   **Volume Profile Arrays:** High Volume Nodes (HVNs) represent areas of price acceptance and consolidation; Low Volume Nodes (LVNs) act as high-speed liquidity voids where price rapidly accelerates [order-flow-absorption-dom](concepts/order-flow-absorption-dom.md).
*   **DOM (Depth of Market) Mechanics:** Watching "Pull and Stack" behavior on the DOM ladder as price approaches an HVN or LVN exposes real institutional intent [order-flow-absorption-dom](concepts/order-flow-absorption-dom.md). Rapid cancellation of resting limit orders ("pulling") indicates no intent to defend, whereas a rapid reload of passive limits ("stacking" or icebergs) confirms active zone defense [order-flow-absorption-dom](concepts/order-flow-absorption-dom.md).

The translation of subjective, discretionary SMC charting into objective, rules-based programmatic execution is accelerating.

*   **Automated Price Action Toolkits:** Platforms like Flux Charts have automated multi-timeframe supply and demand mapping [automated-price-action-toolkits](concepts/automated-price-action-toolkits.md). These tools merge overlapping macro and micro zones and provide no-code visual backtesters on TradingView, screening watchlists for structural CHOCH/BOS shifts in real time [flux-charts-price-action-toolkit](recent/2026-07/flux-charts-price-action-toolkit.md).

### 2. Evolving Risk Rules in Proprietary Trading
Institutional and retail prop firms are enforcing tighter risk parameters to filter out undisciplined traders.
*   **Transition to the "Floating Risk Rule":** Modern proprietary firms are replacing static daily drawdown parameters with dynamic risk engines [prop-firm-mechanics-and-risk.md](concepts/concepts/prop-firm-mechanics-and-risk.md). A trader's maximum loss caps and position-sizing allowances are dynamically compressed or expanded based on real-time market volatility and account equity performance [prop-firm-mechanics-and-risk.md](concepts/concepts/prop-firm-mechanics-and-risk.md).
*   **The Three-Mistake Rule:** Professional risk management is shifting from financial stop-outs to process-oriented circuit breakers [trading-psychology-risk.md](concepts/concepts/trading-psychology-risk.md). This rule mandates closing the trading terminal immediately upon committing three distinct operational execution errors (e.g., over-leveraging, moving a resting stop-loss, or execution outside of session killzones) before a financial daily loss limit is ever violated [trading-psychology-risk.md](concepts/concepts/trading-psychology-risk.md).

## Open Questions / Counter-Evidence

### 1. Backtest Overfitting vs. Live Slippage Friction
While automated backtesters for FVGs, Order Blocks, and BPRs showcase flawless historical equity curves, transitioning to live execution environments frequently exposes severe performance degradation [smc-fvg-ob-mechanics](recent/2026-07/smc-fvg-ob-mechanics.md). Discretionary SMC systems often struggle with lookahead bias, dirty historical data, unmodeled swap/commission costs, and severe execution slippage during high-volatility news releases [smc-fvg-ob-mechanics](recent/2026-07/smc-fvg-ob-mechanics.md).

### 2. Systematic Quantitative Momentum vs. Discretionary Price Action
Quantitative investment firms (such as Wright Research) rely strictly on mathematical, multi-factor momentum and statistical anomalies rather than localized discretionary structure breaks [quantitative-vs-price-action-mechanics](recent/2026-06/concepts/quantitative-vs-price-action-mechanics.md). This poses an ongoing debate: does analyzing micro-structures (like CHOCH or internal inducement) provide a genuine mathematical edge over high-volume portfolio-level momentum, or is retail SMC simply a highly repackaged form of standard trend-following subject to the same statistical limitations?

### 3. Limitations of Multimodal AI Chart Screeners
The emergence of visual AI models (such as SnappChart) designed to analyze static chart screenshots for trading setups has highlighted a major technical bottleneck [market-analysis-technology](recent/2026-07/market-analysis-technology.md). Because visual-only AI models cannot parse live order books, Depth of Market (DOM) liquidity queues, open interest metrics, or real-time macroeconomic calendars, their structural setup grades often lack the broader order-flow context required to survive institutional stop-hunts [market-analysis-technology](recent/2026-07/market-analysis-technology.md).
