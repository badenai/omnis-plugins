## Core Knowledge (by weight)

### 1. Foundational Smart Money Concepts (SMC) & Inner Circle Trader (ICT) Frameworks
*   **Market Structure Hierarchy:** Structural mapping relies on strict, objective definitions of price milestones [market-structure-smc-fundamentals](concepts/concepts/concepts/smc-ict-methodology-framework.md):
    *   **Break of Structure (BOS):** Signals trend continuation. Crucially, a valid BOS requires a *candle body close* beyond the swing high/low. Wick breaks are filtered out and classified strictly as liquidity sweeps or grabs [ict-market-structure-and-liquidity](concepts/ict-market-structure-and-liquidity.md).
    *   **Change of Character (CHoCH) & Market Structure Shift (MSS):** Signals structural trend reversals. A CHoCH represents the initial shift of local/internal structure, while an MSS confirms the institutional shift in order flow on the execution timeframe, validated by energetic displacement [ict-reversal-model-checklist](concepts/concepts/ict-reversal-model-checklist.md).
    *   **Inducement (IDM):** A minor, internal structural break designed to trap early retail breakout traders. A structural trend remains fully intact as long as major external structural swing points are protected, even when internal swings are swept for inducement [market_liquidity_concepts](concepts/concepts/market_liquidity_concepts.md).
*   **Imbalance & Inefficiency (Fair Value Gaps):**
    *   **Fair Value Gap (FVG):** A three-candle price imbalance showing aggressive, single-sided movement where the wicks of the first and third candles do not overlap [ict-killzones-and-fvgs](concepts/ict-killzones-and-fvgs.md).
    *   **BISI vs. SIBI:** Buyside Imbalance Sellside Inefficiency (BISI) represents bullish imbalances; Sellside Imbalance Buyside Inefficiency (SIBI) represents bearish imbalances [ict-smc-framework](concepts/concepts/ict-smc-framework.md).
    *   **Consequent Encroachment (CE):** The exact 50% midpoint of an FVG or a candle wick/shadow, acting as a highly reliable support, resistance, or entry validation level [ict-smc-price-action-concepts](recent/2026-07/ict-smc-price-action-concepts.md).
    *   **Inverse Fair Value Gap (IFVG):** When price decisively closes beyond a pre-existing FVG, the zone flips its structural polarity (bullish FVG becomes bearish resistance, and vice versa) [smc-fvg-ob-mechanics](concepts/concepts/recent/2026-07/smc-fvg-ob-mechanics.md).
    *   **Balanced Price Range (BPR):** A high-precision horizontal overlap of two opposing FVGs (a bullish and a bearish FVG) in the exact same price band, representing immediate algorithmic equilibrium [balanced-price-range-bpr](concepts/balanced-price-range-bpr.md).
*   **Institutional Execution Arrays (Order Blocks):**
    *   **Order Blocks (OB):** The last candle before a decisive, high-volume market expansion, representing institutional order footprints [fvg-and-order-block-confluence](concepts/fvg-and-order-block-confluence.md).
    *   **Breaker Blocks (BB) vs. Mitigation Blocks (MB):** A Breaker is a failed order block formed after price successfully sweeps liquidity (stop hunt of a previous extreme) and completes an MSS. A Mitigation Block is a failed order block born from exhaustion (failure swing) that reverses without sweeping the previous extreme [high-probability-order-blocks.md](concepts/concepts/high-probability-order-blocks.md).
    *   **Hidden Order Blocks (HOB):** Institutional zones disguised within overlapping wicks of consecutive same-color candles on higher timeframes (HTF). Lower timeframe (LTF) drilldowns within these wicks reveal fully formed traditional order blocks [high-probability-order-blocks](concepts/concepts/concepts/high-probability-order-blocks.md).
    *   **Suspension Blocks:** A specialized candlestick body sandwiched between an upper and lower volume imbalance, completely overlapped by a prior wick, preventing standard FVG formation but acting as a dense institutional reload zone [advanced-pd-arrays](concepts/concepts/concepts/advanced-pd-arrays.md).
*   **Premium vs. Discount Valuation:** Every dealing range between a validated swing high and low is split into Premium (top 50%), Equilibrium (50%), and Discount (bottom 50%). High-probability longs are executed strictly in Discount arrays; shorts are executed strictly in Premium arrays [ict-market-structure-and-liquidity](concepts/ict-market-structure-and-liquidity.md).
    *   **Optimal Trade Entry (OTE):** The mathematically optimized zone between the 0.618 and 0.79 retracements of an impulsive structural leg, with 0.705 acting as the sweet-spot entry midpoint [advanced-pd-arrays](concepts/concepts/concepts/advanced-pd-arrays.md).

### 2. Time and Price Theory (Killzones & Algorithmic Cycles)
*   **Daily Algorithmic Timing (Killzones):** High-probability setups are strictly filtered by time [ict-institutional-liquidity-and-timing](concepts/concepts/ict-institutional-liquidity-and-timing.md):
    *   **London Open Killzone:** 2:00 AM – 5:00 AM EST.
    *   **New York Open / Silver Bullet:** 8:30 AM – 11:00 AM EST (with the 10:00 AM – 11:00 AM hour mathematically verified as the single highest-probability macro window) [ict-killzones-and-fvgs](concepts/concepts/ict-killzones-and-fvgs.md).
    *   **New York PM Killzone:** 1:30 PM – 4:00 PM EST.
*   **The Power of Three (AMD):** The daily algorithmic cycle is divided into three distinct phases [ict-smc-framework](concepts/concepts/ict-smc-framework.md):
    *   **Accumulation:** Typically occurs during the low-volume Asian Session, building a tight range of resting orders.
    *   **Manipulation:** Typically occurs during the London Session (often initiating via a *Judas Swing* false breakout) to sweep Asian range liquidity and trap retail traders [ict-kill-zones-and-liquidity-delivery](concepts/ict-kill-zones-and-liquidity-delivery.md).
    *   **Distribution:** Typically occurs during the New York Session, delivering aggressive trend expansion toward opposing liquidity pools using the swept orders as transactional fuel [ict-smc-framework](concepts/concepts/ict-smc-framework.md).
*   **Advanced AMD (AMDX / Quarterly Theory):** Adds a fourth phase, **"X"** (Continuation or Reversal), which mathematically models how the daily or weekly cycle concludes after the primary distribution leg, protecting unrealized gains from late-session algorithmic realignments [amd-power-of-3](concepts/concepts/amd-power-of-3.md).

### 3. Liquidity Engineering & Stop Hunt Mechanics
*   **Liquidity Pools:** Price is algorithmically delivered from one liquidity pool to the next [liquidity-sweeps-and-sentiment-clusters](concepts/liquidity-sweeps-and-sentiment-clusters.md):
    *   **External Liquidity:** Rests outside the current trading range at major, obvious swing highs (buy-side liquidity - BSL) and swing lows (sell-side liquidity - SSL) [market_liquidity_concepts](concepts/concepts/market_liquidity_concepts.md).
    *   **Internal Liquidity:** Sits inside the current range at minor swings, short-term pullbacks, and FVGs.
*   **Equal Highs (EQH) and Equal Lows (EQL):** Form double/triple tops and bottoms. These levels accumulate massive clusters of retail stop losses, making them highly attractive targets for institutional sweeps [liquidity-sweeps-and-pools](concepts/concepts/liquidity-sweeps-and-pools.md).
*   **High Resistance vs. Low Resistance Liquidity:**
    *   **Low Resistance Liquidity:** Forms after a failure swing (price fails to make a new high/low), creating weak, unprotected pools that price easily sweeps [high-low-resistance-liquidity](concepts/concepts/high-low-resistance-liquidity.md).
    *   **High Resistance Liquidity:** Forms after a clean sweep and aggressive structural reversal (e.g., a higher high immediately followed by a lower low). Once high resistance liquidity is swept, price accelerates rapidly toward low resistance pools [high-low-resistance-liquidity](concepts/concepts/high-low-resistance-liquidity.md).

### 4. Volume, Wyckoff Method, & Order Flow Integration
*   **The Wyckoff Method:** Evaluates long-term institutional campaigns through the laws of Supply and Demand, Cause and Effect, and Effort vs. Result [wyckoff-method-and-volume-profile](concepts/concepts/wyckoff-method-and-volume-profile.md).
    *   **Accumulation & Distribution Schematics:** Aligns directly with SMC liquidity cycles, where a Wyckoff "Spring" or "Upthrust" is the structural equivalent of an institutional liquidity sweep [wyckoff-vsa-smc-hybrid](concepts/wyckoff-vsa-smc-hybrid.md).
*   **Volume Profile Analytics:** Translates volume from the time axis to the price axis [wyckoff-market-structure](concepts/wyckoff-market-structure.md):
    *   **Point of Control (POC / VPOC):** The absolute price level with the highest traded volume, acting as a major institutional anchor.
    *   **High Volume Nodes (HVN):** Zones of price acceptance and consolidation where institutions built massive positions [order-flow-absorption-dom](concepts/order-flow-absorption-dom.md).
    *   **Low Volume Nodes (LVN):** High-speed pricing voids/highways where price moves rapidly due to a lack of historical transaction density.
*   **Footprint & Transactional Microstructure:** 
    *   Decomposes standard candles into exact, diagonal bid-ask executed volume [ict-institutional-price-action-mechanics](concepts/concepts/ict-institutional-price-action-mechanics.md).
    *   **Absorption:** Confirmed when massive aggressive market orders hit a level on the footprint, yet price fails to advance due to institutional passive limit orders (icebergs) soaking up the aggression [order-flow-absorption-dom.md](concepts/concepts/order-flow-absorption-dom.md).
    *   **Cumulative Delta Divergence:** Occurs when price prints higher highs while cumulative delta shows decreasing aggression, validating institutional absorption and trend exhaustion [price-volume-delta-divergence](concepts/concepts/price-volume-delta-divergence.md).

*   While retail education advocates placing limit orders blindly at any FVG, a massive **5,400-Gap statistical study** reveals that approximately 30% to 40% of printed FVGs remain entirely unfilled [smc-fvg-ob-mechanics](concepts/concepts/recent/2026-07/smc-fvg-ob-mechanics.md).
*   Furthermore, up to **84% of lower-timeframe (M15) FVGs** that get filled immediately reverse and break structure if they are not backed by a higher-timeframe (H4) order block or a validated premium/discount array [smc-fvg-ob-mechanics](concepts/concepts/recent/2026-07/smc-fvg-ob-mechanics.md). This highlights the danger of trading imbalances in isolation without HTF narrative alignment.

### 2. Discrepancy Between Backtesting and Live Execution
*   Quantitative price action developers continue to grapple with the "performance drift" observed when transitioning from backtest models to live execution [fvg-and-order-block-confluence](concepts/concepts/fvg-and-order-block-confluence.md).
*   This discrepancy is largely driven by lookahead bias in code, dirty historical data, unmodeled transactional and slippage costs, and parameter overfitting (curve-fitting) [recent/2026-07/concepts/concepts/order-flow-volume-analysis.md](recent/2026-07/concepts/concepts/order-flow-volume-analysis.md).

### 3. Subjectivity in Wave Theory & Structural Counts
*   Integrating frameworks like Elliott Wave Theory alongside SMC structures introduces significant analytical friction. Wave counting remains highly subjective; two different professional analysts can construct fully valid, rule-abiding counts on the same chart that point in completely opposite directions [market-structure-and-bias](concepts/concepts/market-structure-and-bias.md).
*   To resolve this, elite operators refuse to use wave counts as standalone entry signals, treating them only as general structural roadmaps that must be confirmed by local liquidity sweeps and market structure shifts [market-structure-and-bias](concepts/concepts/market-structure-and-bias.md).
