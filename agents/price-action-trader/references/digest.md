## Core Knowledge (by weight)

### 1. Market Structure Foundations
*   **Structural Progression:** Objective trend identification is anchored in mapping structural extremes: Higher Highs (HH) and Higher Lows (HL) for bullish regimes; Lower Highs (LH) and Lower Lows (LL) for bearish regimes [market-structure-and-institutional-concepts](recent/2026-07/market-structure-and-institutional-concepts.md).
*   **Trend Transitions & Continues:**
    *   **Break of Structure (BOS):** Signals the continuation of the dominant trend direction [ict-smart-money-concepts](concepts/ict-smart-money-concepts.md).
    *   **Change of Character (CHOCH) / Market Structure Shift (MSS):** Signals a potential structural trend shift [smc-ict-gold-trading-framework](concepts/concepts/concepts/concepts/smc-ict-gold-trading-framework.md).
*   **The Inducement Trap:**
    *   **Internal Inducement (IDM):** A minor, internal structural break designed to trap early retail breakout traders. A true CHOCH requires price to displace and close a full candle body past a major external swing level [structure-inducement-vs-choch](concepts/structure-inducement-vs-choch.md).
    *   **Wicks vs. Closes:** A wick through a structural level is categorized strictly as a liquidity sweep (stop hunt), not a structural break [structure-inducement-vs-choch](concepts/structure-inducement-vs-choch.md).

### 2. Inner Circle Trader (ICT) & Smart Money Concepts (SMC)
*   **Fair Value Gaps (FVG) & Imbalances:**
    *   An FVG is a three-candle price imbalance where aggressive market moves leave an untraded price zone between the first and third candle wicks [ict-killzones-and-imbalances](concepts/ict-killzones-and-imbalances.md).
    *   Sub-structures include **BISI** (Buyside Imbalance Sellside Inefficiency) and **SIBI** (Sellside Imbalance Buyside Inefficiency) [ict-smart-money-foundations](concepts/ict-smart-money-foundations.md).
    *   **Consequent Encroachment (CE):** The exact 50% midpoint of a Fair Value Gap, serving as a highly precise, algorithmic magnet and entry zone [ict-smart-money-foundations](concepts/ict-smart-money-foundations.md).
*   **High-Probability Order Blocks (OB) & Structural Failures:**
    *   **Order Blocks:** Represent the footprint of institutional large orders (the last candle before a decisive, aggressive market expansion) [smc-fvg-ob-mechanics](recent/2026-07/smc-fvg-ob-mechanics.md).
    *   **Breaker Blocks:** A failed order block formed after price successfully sweeps liquidity (stop hunt of a previous extreme) and completes a Market Structure Shift (MSS) [high-probability-order-blocks](concepts/concepts/high-probability-order-blocks.md).
    *   **Mitigation Blocks:** A failed order block formed by a failure swing (exhaustion). Price fails to reach or sweep the previous extreme before reversing through the previous order block [high-probability-order-blocks](concepts/concepts/high-probability-order-blocks.md).
    *   *Operational Distinction:* Breaker blocks contain institutional resting liquidity from swept stop-losses, making them structurally superior to mitigation blocks [high-probability-order-blocks](concepts/concepts/high-probability-order-blocks.md).

### 3. Intra-Session Timing Mechanics (The Power of Three)
*   **Time and Price Theory:** Price action cannot be analyzed in a vacuum; high-probability setups require alignment between key price levels (Premium/Discount arrays) and specific session times [ict-market-structure-and-timing-mechanics](concepts/ict-market-structure-and-timing-mechanics.md).
*   **The Power of Three (AMD):**
    *   **Accumulation (Asian Session):** Low-volume consolidation that builds the liquidity range and maps resting buy/sell stops on both sides [amd-power-of-3](concepts/amd-power-of-3.md).
    *   **Manipulation / Judas Swing (London Session):** A classic institutional stop hunt occurring near session opens, engineering a false run to trap retail traders and sweep Asian range extremes [ict-institutional-price-action-concepts](concepts/ict-institutional-price-action-concepts.md).
    *   **Distribution (New York AM Session):** Price reverses off the manipulation sweep and delivers the true, heavy displacement expansion toward the day's main draw on liquidity [amd-power-of-3](concepts/amd-power-of-3.md).
*   **Session Killzones:** Specific high-volatility time windows where institutional algorithmic order flow peaks:
    *   *London Open Killzone:* 2:00 AM – 5:00 AM ET [ict-killzones-and-imbalances](concepts/ict-killzones-and-imbalances.md).
    *   *New York Open / Silver Bullet:* 8:30 AM – 11:00 AM ET [ict-killzones-and-fvgs](concepts/concepts/ict-killzones-and-fvgs.md).
    *   *New York PM Session:* 1:30 PM – 4:00 PM ET [ict-killzones-and-fvgs](concepts/concepts/ict-killzones-and-fvgs.md).

### 4. Advanced Price Delivery Arrays (PD Arrays)
*   **Inverse Fair Value Gaps (IFVG):** When price closes decisively beyond a standard FVG, the zone flips structural polarity. A breached bullish FVG acts as resistance (bearish IFVG) on subsequent retests [smc-fvg-ob-mechanics](concepts/concepts/recent/2026-07/smc-fvg-ob-mechanics.md).
*   **Balanced Price Range (BPR):** Established when a bullish FVG and a bearish FVG overlap horizontally, representing complete algorithmic equilibrium. The BPR acts as a tight, highly reactive entry zone [balanced-price-range-bpr](concepts/balanced-price-range-bpr.md).
*   **The ICT Unicorn Model:** An A+ entry setup defined as the horizontal overlap of an ICT Breaker Block and an ICT Fair Value Gap (FVG) formed during a displacement leg following a liquidity sweep [unicorn-model-entry](concepts/concepts/unicorn-model-entry.md).
*   **ICT Suspension Block:** A specialized candlestick whose body is completely overlapped by the wick of the prior candle, preventing a standard FVG from forming. Sandwiched between an upper and a lower volume imbalance, it serves as a highly precise reload zone [advanced-pd-arrays](concepts/concepts/concepts/advanced-pd-arrays.md).
*   **Hidden Order Blocks (HOB):** Institutional accumulation/distribution zones hidden within the overlapping wicks of consecutive same-color candles on higher timeframes (HTF). Lower timeframe (LTF) drill-downs within this wick overlap reveal a fully formed, traditional order block [high-probability-order-blocks](concepts/concepts/concepts/high-probability-order-blocks.md).
*   **Institutional Order Flow Entry Drill (IOFED):** A high-momentum market condition where price retraces to the proximal edge of an FVG but fills less than 50% (respecting CE) before aggressively reversing [advanced-pd-arrays](concepts/concepts/advanced-pd-arrays.md).
*   **Optimal Trade Entry (OTE):** A systematic location tool identifying premium/discount dealing ranges. The OTE boundaries are the 0.618 (61.8%) and 0.79 (79%) retracements of the recent impulsive leg, with the 0.705 (70.5%) level serving as the algorithmic sweet spot [advanced-pd-arrays](concepts/concepts/concepts/advanced-pd-arrays.md).

### 5. Wyckoff Method, Volume Profile & Footprint Mechanics
*   **Wyckoff Market Cycles:** Structural categorization based on Accumulation, Re-accumulation, Distribution, and Redistribution phases [wyckoff-vsa-smc-hybrid](concepts/concepts/concepts/wyckoff-vsa-smc-hybrid.md).
*   **Volume Spread Analysis (VSA):** Verifies structural breaks, sweeps, and rejections by evaluating the relationship between price spread, volume, and candlestick wicks [wyckoff-vsa-smc-hybrid](concepts/concepts/concepts/wyckoff-vsa-smc-hybrid.md).
*   **Volume Profile Arrays:**
    *   **High Volume Nodes (HVN):** Areas of heavy historical transaction volume representing price acceptance and consolidation [order-flow-absorption-dom](concepts/order-flow-absorption-dom.md).
    *   **Low Volume Nodes (LVN):** High-speed voids where price accelerates rapidly due to structural order imbalances [order-flow-absorption-dom](concepts/order-flow-absorption-dom.md).
*   **Footprint & DOM Absorption:**
    *   True liquidity sweeps are validated by **absorption** on footprint charts (diagonal Bid x Ask imbalances exceeding a 3x ratio). Unusually massive volume executing at a level without price expansion confirms that passive limit orders are soaking up aggressive market orders [order-flow-absorption-dom](concepts/concepts/order-flow-absorption-dom.md).
    *   **CVD (Cumulative Volume Delta) Divergence:** A key reversal confirmation where price prints a new swing high/low while CVD fails to follow, confirming aggressive order exhaustion [order-flow-absorption-dom](concepts/concepts/order-flow-absorption-dom.md).

## Open Questions / Counter-Evidence

### 1. Backtest vs. Live Performance Drift
*   **Lookahead and Overfitting Bias:** Quantitative reports show a substantial performance discrepancy when transitioning automated SMC/FVG models from historical backtests to live trading environments. This drift is primarily driven by lookahead bias, unmodeled commission/fee structures, execution slippage during rapid sweeps, and curve-overfitting [smc-fvg-ob-mechanics](recent/2026-07/smc-fvg-ob-mechanics.md). Strict live forward-testing with ECN raw spreads (0.0 pips) is required to determine the true execution viability of automated price action strategies.

### 2. Multi-Asset Class Liquidity Discrepancies
*   **Crypto Perps vs. Legacy Futures:** Standard ICT/SMC models were engineered around central-bank-driven FX and legacy futures markets (e.g., ES/NQ). Applying these models directly to highly fragmented, retail-dominated, or offshore crypto perpetual contracts introduces unique risks. Discrepancies in funding rates, open interest spikes, and decentralized exchange liquidity pools can lead to failed sweeps and unmitigated FVG runs that defy classic Time and Price Killzone rules.

### 3. AI Pattern Limitations
*   **Visual-Only Grading Fallacy:** While automated AI chart tools can successfully identify and name geometric price structures (flags, channels, head-and-shoulders), they are fundamentally incapable of reading off-chart context such as live order books, funding rates, open interest, and macroeconomic calendars [market-structure-and-validation](recent/2026-07/market-structure-and-validation.md). Pure price action execution must remain discretionary or hybrid, relying on the trader to filter automated structures through the lens of macro environment and live order flow.
