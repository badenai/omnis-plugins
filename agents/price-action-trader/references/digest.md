**1. Market Structure and Liquidity Mechanics**
*   **The Blueprint of Price Delivery:** Market structure is dictated by a strict sequence of Higher Highs/Higher Lows (HH/HL) and structural breaks. However, to avoid being trapped by retail noise, we must distinguish between internal and external structure. 
*   **CHOCH vs. Inducement (IDM):** A true Change of Character (CHOCH) requires price to close *with displacement* past a major external swing level. If price merely wicks past a level or breaks an internal minor structure, it is categorized as Inducement (IDM)—an algorithmic trap designed to sweep early stop losses before continuing the macro trend [Internal Inducement vs. True CHOCH](concepts/structure-inducement-vs-choch.md).
*   **Liquidity Sweeps (The Catalyst):** High-probability trade setups never occur in a vacuum; they are initiated by a 'Liquidity Sweep' of critical pools, such as Previous Day Highs/Lows (PDH/PDL) or Equal Highs/Lows. We wait for algorithms to purge these Buy-Side Liquidity (BSL) or Sell-Side Liquidity (SSL) pools before validating a reversal [Liquidity Sweep Strategy](concepts/liquidity-sweep-mss-strategy.md).

**2. Institutional Points of Interest (POIs) and Arrays**
*   **High-Probability Order Blocks (OB):** The retail space mislabels every opposing candle as an OB. A valid institutional Order Block must adhere to three mechanical rules: (1) It must initiate a structural break (BOS/CHOCH) that sweeps internal liquidity; (2) It must be followed by aggressive displacement leaving a Fair Value Gap (FVG); (3) It must remain completely unmitigated. Once price wicks into it, the institutional edge is exhausted [High Probability Order Blocks](concepts/high-probability-order-blocks.md).
*   **Balanced Price Range (BPR):** An advanced evolution of the FVG. A BPR forms when an aggressive upward move is instantly countered by aggressive selling, leaving an overlap between a bullish and bearish FVG. This overlapping zone represents a perfectly neutralized "price vacuum." When price returns to a BPR, it acts as an ultra-high-probability, near-zero-drawdown defense level [Balanced Price Range BPR](concepts/balanced-price-range-bpr.md).

**3. Algorithmic Timing and The Power of Three**
*   **AMD (Accumulation, Manipulation, Distribution):** Institutional pricing models follow the AMD cycle based on session timings. The low-volume Asian session accumulates resting orders. The London session engineers the "Judas Swing" (Manipulation) to hunt these stops. The New York session then delivers the true structural expansion (Distribution) targeting unmitigated external liquidity [AMD Power of 3](concepts/amd-power-of-3.md).
