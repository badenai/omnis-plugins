## Core Knowledge (by weight)

### 1. Advanced Market Structure & Intraday Dynamics
*   **Structural Foundations:** High-probability price action trading rejects lagging retail indicators in favor of pure market structure. Trends are identified systematically through the sequence of Higher Highs (HH) and Higher Lows (HL) in bullish regimes, and Lower Highs (LH) and Lower Lows (LL) in bearish regimes [market-structure-and-imbalances](recent/2026-07/market-structure-and-imbalances.md).
*   **Change of Character (CHOCH) vs. Inducement (IDM):** A critical operational error among retail traders is treating minor internal structure shifts as full trend reversals. A valid **Change of Character (CHOCH)** requires price to decisively close with body displacement past a major *external* swing high or low. If price merely penetrates a level with a wick or breaks a minor internal level, it is categorized as **Inducement (IDM)**—a trap engineered by algorithmic order flow to sweep early breakout traders before continuing the higher timeframe trend [structure-inducement-vs-choch](concepts/structure-inducement-vs-choch.md).
*   **Liquidity Sweeps (Stop Hunts):** Markets function as non-random, highly structured liquidity delivery systems. Standard retail technical boundaries (equal highs/lows, session extremes, round numbers) serve as high-density liquidity pools where stop-losses cluster [liquidity-sweep-mss-strategy](concepts/concepts/concepts/liquidity-sweep-mss-strategy.md). Algorithmic price delivery sweeps these pools to absorb opposing retail orders, filling massive institutional positions before reversing [liquidity-sweeps-and-sentiment](concepts/liquidity-sweeps-and-sentiment.md).

### 2. Algorithmic Price Delivery & Key Institutional Arrays
*   **Fair Value Gaps (FVG):** An FVG is a three-candle structural imbalance where the wicks of the first and third candles do not overlap, leaving an untraded price void. These gaps represent aggressive institutional displacement [ict-killzones-and-fvgs](concepts/ict-killzones-and-fvgs.md). Rather than trading every FVG, high-probability execution relies on **nesting**: selecting FVGs that form immediately adjacent to or within a Higher Timeframe (HTF) Order Block [high-probability-order-blocks](concepts/high-probability-order-blocks.md).
*   **Inverse Fair Value Gaps (IFVG):** IFVGs utilize structural polarity flipping. When a standard FVG fails to hold price and experiences a decisive candle close through its boundaries, the zone flips roles (bullish support becomes bearish resistance, and vice versa). This occurs because institutions caught offside are highly incentivized to defend or mitigate their failed positions upon a price retest [smc-fvg-ob-mechanics](concepts/concepts/recent/2026-07/smc-fvg-ob-mechanics.md).
*   **Balanced Price Ranges (BPR):** A BPR is established when a bullish FVG and a bearish FVG horizontally overlap within the same price band, signaling immediate algorithmic rebalancing. Performance metrics demonstrate that BPR entries generated by displacement candles with a **body-to-wick ratio exceeding 70%** yield an average of 2.1R to target, compared to only 1.3R for those under 60% [balanced-price-range-bpr.md](concepts/concepts/balanced-price-range-bpr.md).
*   **Order Blocks (OB):** An Order Block is the last candle before a decisive, impulsive move, leaving an institutional footprint.
    *   *Breaker Blocks:* A failed order block formed after the market sweeps liquidity (stop hunt of a previous extreme) and completes a Market Structure Shift (MSS). Breakers contain intense resting liquidity from swept stops, making them structurally robust support/resistance flips [high-probability-order-blocks.md](concepts/concepts/high-probability-order-blocks.md).
    *   *Mitigation Blocks:* A failed order block formed by a failure swing (exhaustion) where price fails to sweep the previous extreme before reversing. They represent trend fatigue and demand tighter risk parameters [high-probability-order-blocks.md](concepts/concepts/high-probability-order-blocks.md).
    *   *Hidden Order Blocks (HOB):* Accumulation/distribution zones hidden within the overlapping wicks of consecutive same-color candles on a HTF. While invisible on higher intervals, drilling down to a lower timeframe (LTF) inside the wick-overlap reveals a fully formed, unmitigated order block [high-probability-order-blocks](concepts/concepts/concepts/high-probability-order-blocks.md).

### 3. Execution Frameworks & Entry Models
*   **The Unicorn Model:** A mechanical, dual-confirmation reversal setup defined as the horizontal overlap of an **ICT Breaker Block** and an **ICT Fair Value Gap (FVG)**. Following a liquidity sweep and aggressive displacement causing an MSS, an energetic FVG must form directly within the boundaries of the Breaker Block to validate the setup, providing dual structural and imbalance confluence [unicorn-model-entry.md](concepts/concepts/unicorn-model-entry.md).
*   **The Reaper Inversion FVG:** An advanced refinement of the standard Inversion FVG. The Reaper FVG must reside specifically inside the impulsive price leg of a Breaker Block (the leg executing the initial liquidity sweep and MSS). In bullish setups, the Reaper FVG must sit in the discount portion of that leg; in bearish setups, it must sit in the premium portion. It serves as a "hidden" institutional entry zone that mitigates order flow after standard breaker-block traders are swept [unicorn-model-entry](concepts/concepts/concepts/unicorn-model-entry.md).
*   **The Suspension Block:** A specialized candlestick suspended between an upper and a lower volume imbalance, where the candle's body is completely overlapped by the wick of the prior candle to its left. This overlap prevents a standard FVG from forming, yet the body and twin volume imbalances act as a highly precise, low-drawdown algorithmic support/resistance reload zone [advanced-pd-arrays](concepts/concepts/concepts/advanced-pd-arrays.md).
*   **Institutional Order Flow Entry Drill (IOFED):** An aggressive entry technique where a limit order is placed precisely at the proximal edge of an FVG rather than waiting for a 50% consequent encroachment fill. While this carries a higher risk of premature stop-outs during deep retracements, it yields highly asymmetric risk-to-reward ratios in high-momentum markets [iofed-precision-entry](concepts/iofed-precision-entry.md).

### 4. Time, Session Timing, and Volume
*   **Time and Price Theory:** Price levels cannot be analyzed in isolation. Algorithmic institutional order flow is highly time-dependent, executing primarily within specific daily windows [ict-market-structure-and-timing-mechanics](concepts/ict-market-structure-and-timing-mechanics.md).
*   **The Power of Three (AMD):** The daily template maps market delivery through three distinct phases:
    1.  *Accumulation:* The low-volume Asian session consolidates price and builds a tight range of buy-side and sell-side liquidity pools.
    2.  *Manipulation (Judas Swing):* The London session engineers a false breakout (stop hunt) to sweep the Asian range extremes and trap retail participants.
    3.  *Distribution:* The New York AM session heavily displaces in the opposite direction, utilizing swept liquidity to expand toward the unmitigated side of the daily range [amd-power-of-3](concepts/concepts/amd-power-of-3.md).
*   **ICT Kill Zones & Execution Windows:**
    *   *London Open:* 2:00 AM – 5:00 AM ET (Peak volatility/manipulation)
    *   *New York Open / Silver Bullet:* 8:30 AM – 11:00 AM ET (Macro expansion)
    *   *New York PM Session:* 1:30 PM – 4:00 PM ET [ict-killzones-and-fvgs](concepts/ict-killzones-and-fvgs.md)

## Recent Developments (Last 30 Days)

### 1. Volumetric Confirmation of Liquidity Sweeps
Pure price-action models are increasingly being integrated with **Order Flow Footprint Charts** to eliminate lower-timeframe structural lagging. True institutional liquidity sweeps are now verified via **absorption**: a massive surge in market orders (visible as diagonal bid/ask volume imbalances exceeding a 3x ratio) met by passive limit orders, causing price to stall and immediately reverse [order-flow-absorption-dom.md](concepts/concepts/order-flow-absorption-dom.md). If a key high or low is swept but the footprint reveals low aggressive volume alongside negative delta, the breakout lacks institutional backing, confirming a stop hunt is underway [liquidity-sweep-mss-strategy](concepts/concepts/liquidity-sweep-mss-strategy.md).

### 2. Depth of Market (DOM) & Volumetric Polarity
Traders are separating trade location (determined by HTF Volume Profiles and High Volume Nodes) from trade timing (monitored via the DOM ladder). Watching **"pull and stack"** behavior as price approaches key levels reveals institutional intent: the rapid cancellation of resting limit orders ("pull") indicates no intent to defend the zone, while a rapid reload of passive limit orders ("icebergs") confirms real absorption [order-flow-absorption-dom](concepts/order-flow-absorption-dom.md).

### 3. Automated Strategy Validation and Tools
Platforms like Flux Charts are introducing specialized TradingView-integrated suites (Price Action Toolkit and MTF Supply & Demand Zones) to auto-draw structural shifts (BOS, CHOCH), premium/discount arrays, and liquidity pools. Non-coded visual backtesting tools and real-time screeners are being deployed to monitor multiple watchlists simultaneously, reducing manual multi-chart scanning fatigue and preventing emotional overtrading [automated-price-action-toolkits](concepts/automated-price-action-toolkits.md).

## Open Questions / Counter-Evidence

### 1. Static Drawdowns vs. Dynamic "Floating Risk Rules"
Proprietary trading firms are transitioning from traditional static drawdown metrics to algorithmic **"Floating Risk Rules"**. Under these regimes, maximum leverage, daily loss limits, and contract-sizing allowances are dynamically adjusted in real time based on market volatility, account equity, and historical performance metrics. This shift forces funded price action traders to abandon fixed-percentage position sizing in favor of adaptive, contract-based exposure to remain compliant with automated risk engines [prop-firm-mechanics-and-risk.md](concepts/concepts/prop-firm-mechanics-and-risk.md).

### 2. Systematic Backtesting Drift
Recent studies analyze why price action strategies that appear highly profitable in backtesting often experience significant drift and performance degradation in live execution. Primary causes include:
*   Overfitting discretionary rules (such as subjective grading of FVGs and OBs without strict parameters).
*   Failing to model real-world ECN transaction friction, variable spreads, execution latency, and slippage during volatile session openings or liquidity sweep events [market-structure-and-institutional-concepts](recent/2026-07/smc-fvg-and-backtesting-standards.md).

### 3. Discretionary Execution vs. AI-Driven Visual Copilots
The market is experiencing a massive influx of multimodal AI vision models and automated coding tools (such as Pine Script v6 generators utilizing generative AI) designed to screenshot charts and suggest structural setups [automated-volume-profile-execution](concepts/automated-volume-profile-execution.md). However, elite price action purists maintain that visual-only screenshot analysis cannot read live order books, depth of market (DOM), funding rates, or open interest, leaving the critical task of real-time execution context solely in the hands of the human trader [market-structure-and-validation](concepts/market-structure-and-validation.md).
