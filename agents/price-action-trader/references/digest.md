## Core Knowledge (by weight)

### 1. Market Structure & Structural Hierarchy
Market structure forms the foundation of elite price action analysis [ict-smc-market-structure](concepts/ict-smc-market-structure.md). Rather than relying on lagging indicators, price action experts map structural swings strictly utilizing Higher Highs/Higher Lows (HH/HL) and Lower Highs/Lower Lows (LH/LL) [market-structure-and-imbalance-fundamentals](recent/2026-07/market-structure-and-imbalance-fundamentals.md).

An essential discipline of professional market mapping is the strict distinction between structural continuation and structural reversal milestones [ict-market-structure-and-liquidity](concepts/ict-market-structure-and-liquidity.md):
*   **Break of Structure (BOS):** Confirms trend continuation. A valid BOS strictly requires a candle body close past the previous swing extreme [ict-market-structure-and-liquidity](concepts/ict-market-structure-and-liquidity.md). Wicks filtering through a level do not constitute a structural break; they are classified strictly as liquidity sweeps or grabs [ict-market-structure-and-liquidity](concepts/ict-market-structure-and-liquidity.md).
*   **Change of Character (CHOCH):** Signals the first potential trend transition, typically breaking internal structure [ict-market-structure-and-liquidity](concepts/ict-market-structure-and-liquidity.md).
*   **Market Structure Shift (MSS):** Confirms a definitive institutional shift in order flow on the execution timeframe [ict-market-structure-and-liquidity](concepts/ict-market-structure-and-liquidity.md). To validate structural damage and confirm a reversal, an MSS requires strong, energetic price displacement (aggressive momentum candles) [ict-smc-price-action-concepts](recent/2026-07/ict-smc-price-action-concepts.md).

**Premium vs. Discount Pricing Arrays:**
Every trading range between a major swing high and swing low must be divided into:
*   **Premium Zone:** Top 50% of the range [ict-market-structure-and-liquidity](concepts/ict-market-structure-and-liquidity.md).
*   **Equilibrium:** Exactly 50% [ict-market-structure-and-liquidity](concepts/ict-market-structure-and-liquidity.md).
*   **Discount Zone:** Bottom 50% of the range [ict-market-structure-and-liquidity](concepts/ict-market-structure-and-liquidity.md).

High-probability execution dictates that long positions are entered strictly in discount arrays, while short positions are executed strictly in premium arrays [ict-market-structure-and-liquidity](concepts/ict-market-structure-and-liquidity.md). This prevents entering positions in middle-range noise and ensures alignment with institutional wholesale pricing [ict-market-structure-and-liquidity](concepts/ict-market-structure-and-liquidity.md).

### 2. Time-and-Price Theory & Algorithmic Timing
Price cannot be analyzed in a vacuum; high-probability setups require precise alignment between structural price levels and specific macro time windows [ict-market-structure-and-timing-mechanics](concepts/ict-market-structure-and-timing-mechanics.md). Markets are driven by an algorithmic delivery script rather than random movements, delivering price to predetermined liquidity pools and imbalances [ict-kill-zones](concepts/ict-kill-zones.md).

#### Session Kill Zones
These are daily time windows characterized by peak institutional volume, volatility, and algorithmic manipulation [ict-kill-zones](concepts/ict-kill-zones.md):
*   **London Open Kill Zone:** 2:00 AM – 5:00 AM Eastern Time (ET) [ict-kill-zones](concepts/ict-kill-zones.md).
*   **New York Open Kill Zone:** 8:30 AM – 11:00 AM ET [ict-kill-zones](concepts/ict-kill-zones.md).
*   **New York PM Session Kill Zone:** 1:30 PM – 4:00 PM ET [ict-kill-zones](concepts/ict-kill-zones.md).

#### The Silver Bullet Protocol
A highly precise, time-based macro-algorithmic protocol restricted to three rigid one-hour windows [ict-killzones-and-imbalances](concepts/concepts/ict-killzones-and-imbalances.md):
*   **London Open:** 3:00 AM – 4:00 AM EST [ict-killzones-and-imbalances](concepts/concepts/ict-killzones-and-imbalances.md).
*   **New York AM:** 10:00 AM – 11:00 AM EST (the highest-probability macro hour across major currencies, equities, and gold) [ict-killzones-and-imbalances](concepts/concepts/ict-killzones-and-imbalances.md).
*   **New York PM:** 2:00 PM – 3:00 PM EST [ict-killzones-and-imbalances](concepts/concepts/ict-killzones-and-imbalances.md).

Execution within the Silver Bullet window mathematically requires a liquidity sweep to occur prior to or within the initial segment of the hour, followed by an energetic displacement leg that leaves behind a clean Fair Value Gap (FVG) [ict-killzones-and-imbalances](concepts/concepts/ict-killzones-and-imbalances.md).

#### The Judas Swing
An engineered false run (stop hunt) occurring near session opens (specifically London Open), designed to drive price falsely in one direction, sweep resting retail stop-loss clusters, and establish the session's high or low before reversing into the true directional expansion [ict-institutional-price-action-concepts](concepts/ict-institutional-price-action-concepts.md).

#### The Power of 3 (AMD Model)
Models daily candlestick range formulation through three distinct phases [recent/2026-07/ict-macros-and-market-structure](recent/2026-07/ict-macros-and-market-structure.md):
1.  **Accumulation:** High-density consolidation (typically during the low-volume Asian range) building up long and short resting orders [ict-smc-framework](concepts/ict-smc-framework.md).
2.  **Manipulation:** An aggressive algorithmic drive in the opposite direction of the true intended move (the London Judas Swing), sweeping stop losses and trapping retail breakout traders [ict-smc-framework](concepts/ict-smc-framework.md).
3.  **Distribution:** Rapid expansion and trend delivery toward higher-timeframe liquidity targets during the New York sessions [ict-smc-framework](concepts/ict-smc-framework.md).

### 3. Liquidity Pools, Sweeps, & Inducement
Financial markets function as an ongoing auction moving from one liquidity pool to another [concepts/concepts/liquidity-and-sentiment-clusters](concepts/concepts/liquidity-and-sentiment-clusters.md). Institutional participants (Smart Money) require massive counterpart liquidity to execute and distribute their large block positions [concepts/concepts/liquidity-sweeps-and-pools.md](concepts/concepts/liquidity-sweeps-and-pools.md).

*   **External vs. Internal Liquidity:** External liquidity sits outside the current dealing range at major, obvious swing points (Previous Daily High/Low, Session Highs/Lows, High/Low of Day) [concepts/concepts/market_liquidity_concepts.md](concepts/concepts/market_liquidity_concepts.md). Internal liquidity sits inside the current range at minor swing points, short-term pullbacks, and Fair Value Gaps [concepts/concepts/market_liquidity_concepts.md](concepts/concepts/market_liquidity_concepts.md).
*   **Liquidity Inducement (IDM):** A minor, internal structural break designed to trap early breakout traders [structure-inducement-vs-choch](concepts/structure-inducement-vs-choch.md). Algorithms intentionally sweep these internal levels to gather fuel to continue the primary higher-timeframe trend [concepts/concepts/market_liquidity_concepts.md](concepts/concepts/market_liquidity_concepts.md). A structural trend remains completely intact as long as the major external swing low/high is protected, even if internal swing points are heavily swept for inducement [concepts/concepts/market_liquidity_concepts.md](concepts/concepts/market_liquidity_concepts.md).
*   **Liquidity Pools (Stop-Loss Clusters):** Dense areas of retail stop losses resting above equal highs (EQH/double tops) or below equal lows (EQL/double bottoms) [concepts/concepts/liquidity-sweeps-and-pools.md](concepts/concepts/liquidity-sweeps-and-pools.md). These obvious horizontal levels form highly attractive targets for institutional sweeping [concepts/concepts/liquidity-sweeps-and-pools.md](concepts/concepts/liquidity-sweeps-and-pools.md).
*   **Wait-and-Verify Protocol:** In modern, highly algorithmic environments, blind limit orders placed at liquidity pools carry high failure rates [concepts/concepts/concepts/liquidity-sweeps-and-pools](concepts/concepts/concepts/liquidity-sweeps-and-pools.md). High-probability execution requires identifying a higher-timeframe sweep (e.g., 4H/Daily), dropping to a lower execution timeframe (1M/5M), and waiting for a confirmed Market Structure Shift (MSS) or CHOCH [concepts/concepts/concepts/liquidity-sweeps-and-pools](concepts/concepts/concepts/liquidity-sweeps-and-pools.md).

### 4. Refined Price Delivery Arrays (PD Arrays)
Price action targets and entries are framed using specific algorithmic reference points within a dealing range [concepts/concepts/ict-smc-framework](concepts/concepts/ict-smc-framework.md):

#### Fair Value Gaps (FVG)
A three-candle price imbalance created by aggressive institutional displacement where the wicks of the first and third candles do not overlap, leaving an untraded zone of inefficiency [ict-kill-zones](concepts/ict-kill-zones.md).
*   **Consequent Encroachment (CE):** The exact 50% midpoint of an FVG or a long wick/shadow [recent/2026-07/ict-macros-and-market-structure](recent/2026-07/ict-macros-and-market-structure.md). It acts as a critical institutional reaction level and bias confirmation tool [recent/2026-07/institutional-price-action-concepts](recent/2026-07/institutional-price-action-concepts.md).
*   **Inverse Fair Value Gaps (IFVG):** When price closes decisively beyond a bullish FVG, the zone flips structural polarity, acting as immediate bearish resistance on a retest [concepts/concepts/recent/2026-07/smc-fvg-ob-mechanics](concepts/concepts/recent/2026-07/smc-fvg-ob-mechanics.md). Conversely, a breached bearish FVG flips to act as bullish support [concepts/concepts/recent/2026-07/smc-fvg-ob-mechanics](concepts/concepts/recent/2026-07/smc-fvg-ob-mechanics.md). This occurs because institutions caught offside on the initial breach are highly incentivized to mitigate and defend these exact coordinates on a return [concepts/concepts/recent/2026-07/smc-fvg-ob-mechanics](concepts/concepts/recent/2026-07/smc-fvg-ob-mechanics.md).
*   **Balanced Price Range (BPR):** Formed when a bullish FVG and a bearish FVG directly overlap horizontally, representing complete algorithmic price equilibrium [concepts/concepts/balanced-price-range-bpr](concepts/concepts/balanced-price-range-bpr.md). BPR entries are highly reactive, offering tight risk parameters and superior drawdown protection [concepts/concepts/balanced-price-range-bpr](concepts/concepts/balanced-price-range-bpr.md).
*   **Filtros de Calidad:** FVGs or BPRs created by displacement candles with a body-to-wick ratio above 70% demonstrate significantly higher hold rates and yield superior risk-to-reward ratios than those under 60% [concepts/concepts/balanced-price-range-bpr.md](concepts/concepts/balanced-price-range-bpr.md).

#### Order Blocks (OB)
The structural footprint of institutional order execution, representing the last candle of opposite color before a decisive, aggressive market expansion [fvg-and-order-block-confluence](concepts/fvg-and-order-block-confluence.md).
*   **Breaker Blocks:** A failed order block that formed after price successfully swept liquidity (stop hunt of a previous extreme) and completed a Market Structure Shift [concepts/concepts/high-probability-order-blocks.md](concepts/concepts/high-probability-order-blocks.md). Breakers contain heavy institutional resting liquidity from the swept stop-losses, making them structurally robust [concepts/concepts/high-probability-order-blocks.md](concepts/concepts/high-probability-order-blocks.md).
*   **Mitigation Blocks:** A failed order block formed by a failure swing (exhaustion) where price fails to sweep the previous extreme before reversing through the previous order block [concepts/concepts/high-probability-order-blocks.md](concepts/concepts/high-probability-order-blocks.md).
*   **Hidden Order Blocks (HOB):** Institutional accumulation/distribution zones hidden within the overlapping wicks of consecutive same-color candles on higher timeframes [concepts/concepts/concepts/high-probability-order-blocks](concepts/concepts/concepts/high-probability-order-blocks.md). Lower timeframe drilldowns reveal these overlapping wicks as fully formed, high-probability order blocks ignored by basic retail eyes [concepts/concepts/concepts/high-probability-order-blocks](concepts/concepts/concepts/high-probability-order-blocks.md).
*   **Suspension Blocks:** A single candlestick suspended between an upper and a lower volume imbalance, where the body is completely overlapped by the wick of the prior candle, preventing a standard FVG from forming [concepts/concepts/concepts/concepts/advanced-pd-arrays](concepts/concepts/concepts/concepts/advanced-pd-arrays.md). This acts as a highly reliable support/resistance zone where the institutional algorithm reloads orders [concepts/concepts/concepts/concepts/advanced-pd-arrays](concepts/concepts/concepts/concepts/advanced-pd-arrays.md).
*   **Nesting Principle:** High-probability execution requires nesting. The most reliable FVGs are those directly nested within, or immediately adjacent to, a Higher Timeframe (HTF) Order Block [concepts/high-probability-order-blocks](concepts/high-probability-order-blocks.md).

#### High-Precision Entry Models
*   **The Unicorn Model:** The horizontal overlap of a Breaker Block and an FVG, creating a rare triple-confluence zone (Sweep + MSS + Overlapping PD Arrays) [concepts/concepts/concepts/concepts/unicorn-model-entry.md](concepts/concepts/concepts/concepts/unicorn-model-entry.md).
*   **The Venom Model:** Executing immediately off the boundaries of a Balanced Price Range (BPR) formed directly after a major liquidity sweep inside a session Kill Zone [concepts/unicorn-model-entry](concepts/unicorn-model-entry.md).
*   **Optimal Trade Entry (OTE):** Location tool defined by the 0.618 and 0.79 Fibonacci retracements of a validated structural impulsive leg, with the 0.705 level serving as the algorithmic sweet spot [concepts/concepts/concepts/concepts/advanced-pd-arrays.md](concepts/concepts/concepts/concepts/advanced-pd-arrays.md). Target projections are set symmetrically at the -0.27 and -0.62 extensions [concepts/concepts/concepts/concepts/advanced-pd-arrays.md](concepts/concepts/concepts/concepts/advanced-pd-arrays.md).

### 5. Footprint, Volume spread, & Wyckoff Analysis
To confirm price action structure, elite traders utilize order flow, Volume Profile, and Auction Market Theory [concepts/wyckoff-market-structure](concepts/wyckoff-market-structure.md).

*   **Auction Market Theory:** Price acts strictly as an advertising mechanism to attract business, Time regulates opportunity, and Volume measures the success of the auction [concepts/concepts/concepts/market_liquidity_concepts](concepts/concepts/concepts/market_liquidity_concepts.md). Price moves when one side becomes highly aggressive and willing to transact at less favorable prices, creating imbalance [concepts/concepts/concepts/market_liquidity_concepts](concepts/concepts/concepts/market_liquidity_concepts.md).
*   **Volume Profile Structure:** Maps volume to the Y-axis (price), revealing the Point of Control (POC - absolute price level with highest volume), High Volume Nodes (HVNs - representing price acceptance and consolidation), and Low Volume Nodes (LVNs - dynamic liquidity highways where price accelerates due to lack of historical transactions) [concepts/concepts/order-flow-volume-profile](concepts/concepts/order-flow-volume-profile.md).
*   **Footprint & Footprint Imbalances:** Footprint cells are read diagonally (Bid x Ask) to track aggressive market order execution [concepts/concepts/order-flow-absorption-dom.md](concepts/concepts/order-flow-absorption-dom.md). Buying or selling imbalances are marked when one side exceeds the other by a 3x ratio [concepts/concepts/order-flow-absorption-dom.md](concepts/concepts/order-flow-absorption-dom.md).
*   **Absorption Mechanics:** Occurs when unusually massive market order volume trades on both sides of a footprint cell, yet price fails to advance, indicating passive limit order walls are completely soaking up aggressive market orders [concepts/concepts/order-flow-absorption-dom.md](concepts/concepts/order-flow-absorption-dom.md). Absorption is structurally verified when a candle closes bullish but the net Delta below the bar is negative, proving aggressive sellers were absorbed by a passive buy wall [concepts/concepts/order-flow-absorption-dom.md](concepts/concepts/order-flow-absorption-dom.md).
*   **Wyckoff Integration:** SMC and ICT concepts are streamlined, modernized dialects of classical Wyckoff Theory [concepts/concepts/concepts/ict-smc-framework](concepts/concepts/concepts/ict-smc-framework.md). The SMC "Liquidity Sweep" is the structural equivalent of a Wyckoff "Spring" or "Upthrust," while the "Change of Character" acts as the Wyckoff "Sign of Strength" (SoS) or "Sign of Weakness" (SoW) [concepts/wyckoff-vsa-smc-hybrid](concepts/wyckoff-vsa-smc-hybrid.md).

### 6. Professional Risk Mechanics & Position Sizing
Strict risk management and position sizing represent the ultimate safeguards against market ruin [concepts/concepts/risk-management-and-position-sizing](concepts/concepts/risk-management-and-position-sizing.md).

*   **Fixed Fractional Sizing:** Risking a constant percentage of capital (typically 1% to 2%) per trade, dynamically scaling contract sizes so that the monetary risk remains identical regardless of the physical size of the stop loss [concepts/concepts/risk-management-and-position-sizing](concepts/concepts/risk-management-and-position-sizing.md).
*   **Volatility-Based Sizing:** Scaling trade size inversely with market volatility; higher volatility mandates wider structural stop losses and smaller position sizes, while lower volatility allows tighter stops and larger positions [concepts/concepts/risk-management-and-position-sizing](concepts/concepts/risk-management-and-position-sizing.md).
*   **Stop-Loss Placement:** Stop-losses must be mechanically positioned entirely past the extreme distal line of the sweeping candle or the invalidation point of the parent Order Block, rather than tight FVG boundaries, to prevent being prematurely hunted by minor liquidity sweeps [concepts/concepts/ict-killzones-and-imbalances](concepts/concepts/ict-killzones-and-imbalances.md).
*   **Trade Management Rules:** Reduce risk by 50% upon price reaching 1R [recent/2026-07/recent/price-action-mechanics](recent/2026-07/recent/price-action-mechanics.md). Move stops to Break-Even (BE) at 2R, and trail aggressively past 2R into structural targets [recent/2026-07/recent/price-action-mechanics](recent/2026-07/recent/price-action-mechanics.md).

### 1. Subjectivity vs. Algorithmic Automation in Wave and Structure Counting
A prominent debate continues regarding the implementation of multi-timeframe structural frameworks, such as Elliott Wave Theory and standard SMC structural counts [concepts/concepts/market-structure-and-bias](concepts/concepts/market-structure-and-bias.md). Critics highlight that wave counting and structural identification remain highly subjective in real-time execution compared to historical hindsight [concepts/concepts/market-structure-and-bias](concepts/concepts/market-structure-and-bias.md). Two professional analysts can build entirely valid, rule-abiding counts on the same chart that point in opposite directions [concepts/concepts/market-structure-and-bias](concepts/concepts/market-structure-and-bias.md). To mitigate this subjectivity, systematic traders increasingly rely on algorithmic Python packages (such as the open-source `smart-money-concepts` library) to codify structure breaks, order blocks, and premium/discount ranges mathematically, removing human emotional bias from the charting process [concepts/concepts/algorithmic-smart-money-concepts-python](concepts/concepts/algorithmic-smart-money-concepts-python.md).

### 2. Cumulative Volume Delta (CVD) Divergence Misinterpretations
Many retail order flow guides heavily promote Cumulative Volume Delta (CVD) divergences as immediate reversal signals [concepts/concepts/wyckoff-methodology-and-volume-profile](concepts/concepts/wyckoff-methodology-and-volume-profile.md). However, quantitative analysis shows CVD divergences (such as negative CVD during a price rise) are frequently normal absorption signatures of passive institutional buyers (utilizing limit orders to consume aggressive retail market sells) rather than warnings of an imminent reversal [concepts/concepts/wyckoff-methodology-and-volume-profile](concepts/concepts/wyckoff-methodology-and-volume-profile.md). Consequently, relying strictly on session-wide CVD lines introduces massive execution noise [concepts/concepts/wyckoff-methodology-and-volume-profile](concepts/concepts/wyckoff-methodology-and-volume-profile.md). Order flow execution requires filtering CVD signals through per-cell footprint delta profiles, confirming localized passive limit order absorption at exact High Volume Nodes (HVNs) before executing structural reversals [concepts/concepts/wyckoff-methodology-and-volume-profile](concepts/concepts/wyckoff-methodology-and-volume-profile.md).
