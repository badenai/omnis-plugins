## Core Knowledge (by weight)

### 1. The Unified ICT & Smart Money Concepts (SMC) Framework
The foundation of institutional price action trading relies on the understanding that price is not random; it is driven by algorithmic delivery to pools of liquidity. 
*   **The Daily Algorithmic Script (Power of Three / AMD):** Markets operate in a daily cycle engineered to trap retail capital. This cycle consists of:
    *   **Accumulation:** Occurring primarily during the low-volume Asian session (8 PM - 12 AM EST), this phase builds a tight consolidation range, generating resting buy-side liquidity (BSL) and sell-side liquidity (SSL) pools above and below the range.
    *   **Manipulation (Judas Swing):** Executed during the London session (2 AM - 5 AM EST), this is a false expansion designed to trigger stop losses and trick breakout traders. It sweeps the Asian range extremes.
    *   **Distribution:** Initiated in the New York AM session (7 AM - 12 PM EST), where institutional orders drive the true directional expansion in the opposite direction of the London manipulation, targeting unmitigated high-timeframe zones.
    *   **Reversal/Continuation (London Close):** Taking place from 10 AM - 12 PM EST, serving as profit-taking or macro realignments.
*   **Market Structure Hierarchy:** Structural identification is governed by Higher Highs / Higher Lows (HH/HL) in bullish trends, and Lower Highs / Lower Lows (LH/LL) in bearish trends. True market reversals require a clear **Market Structure Shift (MSS)** or **Change of Character (CHOCH)** with displacement (strong candle body closes) beyond key swing points, rather than a mere wick.
*   **Imbalances & Fair Value Gaps (FVGs):** An FVG occurs when a three-candle sequence shows rapid displacement, leaving a gap where only one side of the market (buyers or sellers) was matched.
    *   **BISI:** Buyside Imbalance Sellside Inefficiency (bullish gap).
    *   **SIBI:** Sellside Imbalance Buyside Inefficiency (bearish gap).
    *   **Consequent Encroachment (CE):** The exact 50% midpoint of an FVG, which acts as a major institutional support/resistance level.
*   **Premium vs. Discount Arrays:** Trading must only occur in alignment with institutional pricing. Using a Fibonacci framework over a dealing range, buy-side executions are strictly limited to the **Discount** zone (below 50% equilibrium), and sell-side executions to the **Premium** zone (above 50% equilibrium).

*For deeper concepts, refer to the [ICT SMC Framework](concepts/ict-smc-framework.md) and the [Power of Three (AMD)](concepts/amd-power-of-3.md).*

### 2. High-Probability Order Blocks & Structure Validation
An Order Block (OB) is not merely "the last opposing candle." It represents localized institutional accumulation or distribution.
*   **Three Mechanical Rules of OB Validation:**
    1.  **Market Structure Alignment:** The block must originate the move that creates a Break of Structure (BOS) or CHOCH on the higher timeframe.
    2.  **Aggressive Displacement:** The resulting impulse leg must immediately leave behind a Fair Value Gap (FVG), proving institutional urgency and an imbalance of unfilled orders.
    3.  **Unmitigated State:** Only the very first touch of a fresh, unmitigated order block holds a high-probability edge. Once mitigated, the resting institutional blocks are filled and the edge is compromised.
*   **Breaker Blocks vs. Mitigation Blocks:** 
    *   **Breaker Blocks (High-Probability):** A broken order block that successfully **swept liquidity** (engineered a stop hunt making a new higher high or lower low) before the structural shift occurred. This confirms "weak hands" were purged and institutional capital is fully committed, turning the block into a highly reliable support/resistance flip zone.
    *   **Mitigation Blocks (Lower-Probability):** A broken order block where price failed to sweep liquidity (created a failure swing, i.e., lower high or higher low) before breaking structure. While still a valid zone representing a shift in flow, it lacks the aggressive liquidity fuel of a Breaker.

*For complete validation criteria, refer to [High-Probability Order Blocks](concepts/high-probability-order-blocks.md).*

### 3. The Unicorn and Venom Entry Models
Elite price action executions rely on overlapping high-probability structural variables to minimize drawdown and mathematical risk.
*   **The Unicorn Model:** This premier entry setup requires the **horizontal overlap of a Breaker Block and a Fair Value Gap (FVG)** within a premium or discount zone. Because the Breaker carries the "rocket fuel" of a completed liquidity sweep and the FVG represents urgent displacement, their horizontal convergence forms a highly sensitive price magnet. Entries are placed at the proximal boundary of the FVG with tight stops tucked behind the Breaker's structural swing.
*   **The Venom Model:** A simplified, high-velocity entry model that strictly utilizes the **Balanced Price Range (BPR)** formed immediately after a major liquidity sweep during predetermined session Killzones.

### 4. Balanced Price Range (BPR) Equilibrium
A Balanced Price Range (BPR) is an advanced premium/discount array characterized as a "double Fair Value Gap" horizontal overlap.
*   **Structural Formation:** A BPR forms when an aggressive, displaced move in one direction (creating an FVG) is immediately overrun and invalidated by an aggressive, counter-displaced move in the opposite direction (creating a second, opposing FVG over the exact same price zone).
*   **Market Mechanics:** Because both buy-side and sell-side imbalances have occupied the exact same price range in a highly compressed window, the market has cleared both sides of the book. The overlap represents a temporary state of perfect institutional equilibrium. When price subsequently returns to this zone, institutions defend it aggressively, resulting in highly precise, near-zero-drawdown rejections.

### 5. Footprint Chart Integration and Micro-Order Flow Absorption
Relying solely on candlestick charts can introduce execution lag. Blending smart money levels with footprint volume charts validates institutional activity in real time.
*   **Absorption Setups:** When price reaches a key HTF level (e.g., an Order Block or liquidity sweep point), footprints reveal if the level is holding. True absorption is confirmed when a massive surge in market orders (visible as a large diagonal buy/sell imbalance or spike in aggressive delta) fails to push price forward, indicating that passive institutional limit orders are absorbing the volume.
*   **Initiation Confirmation:** Following absorption, "Initiation" is validated via delta divergence. This occurs when aggressive buying/selling imbalances form in the opposite direction of the swept level, proving smart money has completed accumulation and is actively pushing price away.
*   **Stacked Imbalances:** Identified when three or more consecutive price ticks within a footprint candle show diagonal buying or selling imbalances exceeding 300%. These zones act as solid dynamic support/resistance zones.
*   **Unfinished Auctions:** Occur when bid/ask volumes fail to print zero at the extreme highs or lows of a candle, indicating the auction was cut off by time. Price is highly likely to return to resolve and close these levels later in the session.

*For order flow execution mechanics, see [Order Flow Absorption & DOM](concepts/order-flow-absorption-dom.md).*

Integrating Wyckoff phases with algorithmic liquidity concepts bridges the gap between decentralized forex volume proxies and on-chart structural sweeps.
*   **The Liquidity Equivalence:** Classical Wyckoff events are mapped directly onto modern liquidity sweeps:
    *   A Wyckoff **"Spring"** is functionally a Sell-Side Liquidity (SSL) sweep below a consolidation range.
    *   A Wyckoff **"Upthrust" (UT/UTAD)** is a Buy-Side Liquidity (BSL) sweep above a range.
*   **Tick Volume (VSA Proxy):** In decentralized OTC markets like Forex, Tick Volume serves as a highly accurate proxy for institutional effort. An expansion of tick volume during a structural sweep (Spring/Upthrust) followed by an immediate wick rejection and structural close back inside the range confirms institutional accumulation rather than genuine expansion.

### 7. Core Liquidity Sweeps vs. Inducement
Distinguishing between a true structural shift and a trap is a critical skill for preserving capital.
*   **Liquidity Sweeps:** Occur when price purges key high-timeframe structural points (Previous Day High/Low, Weekly High/Low, Session High/Low) and immediately closes back within the range, creating long wick rejections.
*   **Structure Inducement (IDM):** A minor, internal structural break designed to trap early retail trend-followers and breakout traders. 
*   **The Crucial Filter:** A true Change of Character (CHOCH) or Market Structure Shift (MSS) requires price to displace and close a full candle body beyond a major, *external* swing level. If price merely breaks an internal, minor structure, it is categorized as Inducement—an engineered trap swept by algorithms before resuming the higher-timeframe trend.

```
[Retail Breakout Trap]  ---> Price breaks internal high (No body close) ---> Swept as Inducement
[True Institutional MSS] ---> Price aggressively closes candle body past external high ---> Valid Shift
```

*For structural rules, refer to [Structure Inducement vs CHOCH](concepts/structure-inducement-vs-choch.md) and [Liquidity Sweep MSS Strategy](concepts/liquidity-sweep-mss-strategy.md).*

## Open Questions / Counter-Evidence

### 1. The Unicorn Model Definition Split
There is an ongoing debate regarding the exact structural requirements of the "Unicorn Model."
*   **The Institutional Standard:** Advanced SMC practitioners state that a high-probability Unicorn requires the overlapping confluence of a **Breaker Block** (which swept liquidity) and an FVG.
*   **The Lowered Retail Standard:** Diverse retail educational resources have diluted this standard, defining the model as an overlap between a **Mitigation Block** (which did not sweep liquidity) and an FVG. 
*   *Counter-Evidence:* Quantitative testing indicates that Mitigation-based Unicorn models suffer from a significantly lower win rate and higher drawdown. This occurs because Mitigation blocks lack the "rocket fuel" of a cleared liquidity pool (stop hunt), making them standard continuation patterns rather than true institutional traps.

### 2. Discretionary SMC vs. Systematic Quantitative Momentum Models
The core premise of discretionary price action is that reacting to localized liquidity sweeps and market structure shifts is superior to mathematical modeling.
*   **Quantitative Factor Investing:** Institutions (such as Wright Research) successfully deploy AI-powered quantitative multi-factor and momentum models. These models bypass manual chart patterns (like order blocks or BPRs) entirely, managing multi-million dollar portfolios purely on mathematical anomalies and statistical trend continuations across thousands of assets.
*   *The Conflict:* Can localized SMC setups consistently outperform broad-scale statistical factor models, or do retail SMC traders fall victim to cognitive bias, overestimating their ability to manually read algorithmic price delivery?

### 3. Broker Capital Protection Schemes (MondShield / Loss Coverage)
Modern ECN brokers have introduced capital protection systems (e.g., MondShield) claiming up to 60% loss coverage on qualifying drawdowns.
*   *The Skepticism:* From an institutional standpoint, there is no free lunch in risk management. Are these features genuine risk-mitigation tools designed to protect serious traders, or are they marketing gimmicks engineered to encourage higher leverage and risk-taking behavior among retail participants?

### 4. Automated SMC Toolkits vs. Manual Multi-Timeframe Synthesis
The proliferation of automated SMC indicator suites (e.g., LuxAlgo, Flux Charts) has made structural charting accessible to the retail public.
*   *The Risk:* Institutional pricing algorithms operate dynamically across multiple timeframes. Automated scripts often struggle to accurately identify the dominant higher-timeframe trend context, frequently labeling internal inducement wicks as true CHOCH signals. Over-reliance on automated tools can lead to analysis paralysis, curve-fitting in backtests, and high-frequency execution traps. Detailed manual top-down analysis remains the primary edge of the professional price action trader.
