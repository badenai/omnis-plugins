# Price Action Expert Soul: Comprehensive Memory Document

## Core Knowledge (by weight)

### 1. Foundational Market Structure & Dynamic Shifts
Market structure is the bedrock of objective, non-lagging price action analysis. True trends and ranges are mapped out by identifying structural extremes: Higher Highs (HH), Higher Lows (HL), Lower Highs (LH), and Lower Lows (LL) [concepts/concepts/core-market-structure-mechanics.md]. 
*   **Break of Structure (BOS):** Signals the continuation of an established trend.
*   **Change of Character (CHOCH) / Market Structure Shift (MSS):** Signals a potential trend reversal. However, a lower-timeframe CHOCH is highly prone to noise unless it occurs directly inside a Higher Timeframe (HTF) Point of Interest (POI), such as a 4H or Daily Order Block or Fair Value Gap [concepts/concepts/concepts/ict-market-structure-and-liquidity.md]. 
*   **Internal Inducement (IDM) vs. True Reversals:** Inducement is a minor internal structural break or sweep designed to trap breakout traders. A true CHOCH requires price to close *with body displacement* past a major external swing level. Minor wicks through external structures are classified as liquidity sweeps (stop hunts), not structural trend shifts [concepts/structure-inducement-vs-choch.md].

### 2. Algorithmic Liquidity & Sentiment Dynamics
Markets are non-random; they operate on a structured sequence that delivers price to predictable liquidity pools.
*   **External vs. Internal Liquidity:** External liquidity rests outside the active trading range at major swing highs (Buy-Side Liquidity/BSL) and swing lows (Sell-Side Liquidity/SSL). Internal liquidity resides inside the current range at minor swing points, short-term pullbacks, and Fair Value Gaps. Typically, internal liquidity is swept for inducement before the market seeks major external pools [concepts/concepts/market_liquidity_concepts.md].
*   **Equal Highs (EQH) & Equal Lows (EQL):** Double or triple tops/bottoms accumulate high-density retail stop-loss clusters (buy stops above, sell stops below). These form highly attractive targets for institutional sweeping, providing the "fuel" needed to execute massive institutional orders [concepts/concepts/liquidity-sweeps-and-pools.md].
*   **Low Resistance vs. High Resistance Liquidity:** Low resistance liquidity pools form after a failure swing (a failure to make a higher high/lower low). High resistance liquidity is established after a clean sweep and structural reversal. Once high resistance liquidity is taken, price accelerates easily toward low resistance pools, following the path of least resistance [concepts/concepts/high-low-resistance-liquidity.md].
*   **Sentiment as a Contrarian Filter:** Lopsided retail sentiment extremes (e.g., >65% long or short) signal crowded trades and trend exhaustion. Waiting for a physical sweep of these retail stop-loss clusters, combined with lower-timeframe structural verification, yields highly asymmetric reversal setups [concepts/concepts/liquidity-sweeps-and-sentiment-extremes.md].

### 3. Institutional Footprints: Gaps, Blocks, and Arrays
Institutions leave footprints on price charts. The professional trading engine uses these footprints rather than lagging retail indicators.
*   **Fair Value Gaps (FVG):** Three-candle imbalances leaving an untraded zone between the first and third candle wicks (Buy-Side Imbalance Sell-Side Inefficiency [BISI] or Sell-Side Imbalance Buy-Side Inefficiency [SIBI]) [concepts/concepts/ict-smc-framework.md]. High-probability FVGs are those directly nested within or adjacent to an HTF Order Block. Gaps should be filtered by the body-to-wick ratio of their displacement candles, requiring ratios above 70% for optimal R:R [concepts/concepts/balanced-price-range-bpr.md].
*   **Inverse Fair Value Gaps (IFVG):** When price closes decisively beyond the boundary of an active FVG, the zone flips polarity (bullish FVG becomes bearish resistance, and vice versa). Market makers are highly incentivized to defend these offside positions upon a retest [concepts/concepts/recent/2026-07/smc-fvg-ob-mechanics.md].
*   **Balanced Price Ranges (BPR):** Established when a bullish FVG and a bearish FVG overlap horizontally. This represents absolute algorithmic equilibrium and serves as a highly reactive mitigation magnet with clean invalidation boundaries [concepts/concepts/balanced-price-range-bpr.md].
*   **Order Blocks (OB):** The last candle before a decisive structural market expansion. High-probability setups target unmitigated OBs in premium or discount zones [concepts/fvg-and-order-block-confluence.md].
*   **Breaker Blocks vs. Mitigation Blocks:** A Breaker Block is a failed order block formed after price sweeps liquidity (stop hunt of a previous extreme) and completes an MSS. A Mitigation Block is a failed order block formed by a failure swing (exhaustion), meaning price did not sweep the previous extreme. Breakers are structurally stronger due to the resting institutional liquidity swept in the process [concepts/concepts/high-probability-order-blocks.md].
*   **The ICT Unicorn Model:** An A+ setup defined as the direct horizontal overlap of a Breaker Block and an active Fair Value Gap [concepts/concepts/unicorn-model-entry.md].
*   **Premium vs. Discount Arrays & OTE:** Trading ranges must be split into Premium (expensive/sells) and Discount (cheap/buys) relative to the 50% equilibrium. Optimal Trade Entry (OTE) utilizes the 62% to 79% Fibonacci retracement band, with 70.5% acting as the algorithmic sweet spot [concepts/concepts/concepts/advanced-pd-arrays.md].

### 4. Algorithmic Time & Price Theory
Timing is the ultimate execution filter. Setup validation requires the alignment of high-probability pricing arrays with specific daily session windows.
*   **Kill Zones:** Intraday algorithmic volume injections occur in specific windows: London Open (2:00–5:00 AM ET), New York Open (8:30–11:00 AM ET), and the New York PM Session (1:30–4:00 PM ET) [concepts/concepts/ict-core-concepts.md].
*   **The Power of Three (AMD):** The daily algorithmic cycle: Accumulation (Asian consolidation range), Manipulation (London Judas Swing—a false run sweeping Asian highs or lows), and Distribution (New York session expansion executing the true daily trend) [concepts/concepts/ict-smart-money-foundations.md].
*   **The Silver Bullet Windows:** Rigid 60-minute macro-algorithmic windows: London (3:00–4:00 AM EST), New York AM (10:00–11:00 AM EST), and New York PM (2:00–3:00 PM EST). High-probability execution requires a liquidity sweep immediately preceding or occurring in the first minutes of the window, followed by a displacement leg leaving a clean FVG [concepts/concepts/ict-killzones-and-imbalances.md].

### 5. Advanced Order Flow & Volumetric Verification
SMC charting concepts are highly optimized when bridged with transactional order flow realities.
*   **Footprints & Depth of Market (DOM):** Footprint charts act as an X-ray of candlesticks, displaying exact diagonal (Bid x Ask) transaction sizes. True institutional sweeps are confirmed by "absorption"—where a massive surge of market orders (diagonal delta spikes) is met by passive limit orders, stalling and immediately reversing price rather than expanding [concepts/concepts/liquidity-sweep-mss-strategy.md].
*   **Volume Profile Structure:** Identifies High Volume Nodes (HVNs) where historical value was accepted (acting as magnet/support/resistance zones) and Low Volume Nodes (LVNs) where price moves rapidly due to a lack of resting orders [concepts/concepts/order-flow-volume-profile.md].
*   **Cumulative Volume Delta (CVD) Divergence:** A common retail trap is trading simple CVD line divergence. Professional order flow execution prioritizes per-cell footprint delta profiles over cumulative delta lines, verifying where buying or selling aggression is concentrated inside structural extremes [concepts/concepts/continuation-acceleration-protocol-mechanics.md].

## Open Questions / Counter-Evidence

*   **discrepancy between Backtesting & Live Slippage:** Systematic backtests of over 5,400 Fair Value Gaps indicate a high mathematical edge, yet traders face execution drift in live environments. This performance gap is driven by lookahead biases in historical data, unmodeled transaction costs, and real-time execution slippage during rapid liquidity sweeps [recent/2026-07/concepts/concepts/order-flow-volume-analysis.md].
*   **AI Vision Limitations in Chart Analysis:** While multimodal AI tools are being developed to grade static chart screenshots, they are fundamentally blind to crucial live-market context (order books, open interest, funding rates, and real-time news delivery). Naming a textbook pattern is non-predictive; risk management and reacting to live order book data remain the human trader's absolute responsibility [recent/2026-07/market-structure-and-execution.md].
*   **Subjectivity of Elliott Wave and Sentiment Models:** Standard wave counting rules (such as Wave 4 not overlapping Wave 1) are highly objective in hindsight, but real-time counting remains highly subjective. Two different analysts can create opposing, rule-compliant wave counts on the same chart, emphasizing why macro roadmaps must always be secondary to immediate price action rejections at local liquidity pools [concepts/concepts/market-structure-and-bias.md].
