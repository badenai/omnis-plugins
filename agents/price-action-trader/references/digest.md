## Core Knowledge (by weight)

As an elite price action trader, my methodology is rooted entirely in pure price mechanics, institutional order flow, and structural alignment. The following concepts form the non-negotiable foundation of my trading model, weighted by probabilistic reliability.

**1. Market Structure & Liquidity Sweeps (Relevance: 1.0)**
True institutional edge begins with a liquidity hunt. High-probability setups originate from a sweep of external liquidity—specifically Previous Day Highs/Lows (PDH/PDL) or established session boundaries. A valid reversal requires a violent Market Structure Shift (MSS) accompanied by displacement, proving institutional footprint. Trade execution occurs upon retracement into the resulting [Fair Value Gap (FVG)](concepts/liquidity-sweep-mss-strategy.md). Risk is mechanically managed by placing stops strictly beyond the wick of the liquidity sweep to protect the structural level.

**2. Balanced Price Range (BPR) Equilibrium (Relevance: 1.0)**
The [Balanced Price Range](concepts/balanced-price-range-bpr.md) is the pinnacle of price vacuums. It occurs when an aggressive upward move is immediately reversed by aggressive selling (or vice versa), creating overlapping bullish and bearish FVGs. This overlap represents a perfect state of equilibrium where supply and demand are neutralized. Once broken, BPRs act as ultra-high-probability, zero-drawdown institutional defense zones that validate true structural shifts over mere liquidity grabs.

**3. Mechanical Order Block (OB) Validation (Relevance: 1.0)**
Retail traders label any opposing candle as an Order Block, leading to consistent failure. A valid, high-probability [Order Block](concepts/high-probability-order-blocks.md) must satisfy three strict, mechanical rules:
1. It must originate a structural break (BOS/CHOCH) that sweeps *internal* liquidity.
2. It must be immediately followed by aggressive displacement that leaves an FVG.
3. It must be 100% unmitigated (the first touch is the only tradable event).

**4. The Unicorn Model Confluence (Relevance: 0.98)**
The [Unicorn Model](concepts/unicorn-model-entry.md) is a premier entry framework created by overlapping a Higher Timeframe Breaker Block or Order Block with an FVG. This "double confirmation" zone stacks institutional historical entry points with price vacuums, allowing for aggressive execution and eliminating the need for secondary lower-timeframe confirmation. It rapidly delivers 1:2 or 1:3 R:R.

**5. Order Flow Absorption & True CHOCH vs. Inducement (Relevance: 0.95)**
Relying purely on naked charts is enhanced by integrating DOM and Footprint data to identify [Order Flow Absorption](concepts/order-flow-absorption-dom.md). Sweeps are validated when heavy institutional limit orders (liquidity walls) absorb aggressive retail market orders, trapping them before the reversal. 
Furthermore, differentiating between a [True CHOCH and Inducement (IDM)](concepts/structure-inducement-vs-choch.md) is critical. A true CHOCH requires price to close *with displacement* past a major external swing level. Mere wicks are stop hunts. Internal, minor structural breaks are algorithmic traps (IDM) engineered to wipe out early breakout traders.

**6. Wyckoff / SMC Convergence (Relevance: 0.95)**
Classical Volume Spread Analysis (VSA) is perfectly mapped to SMC. A Wyckoff "Spring" is mathematically a Sell-Side Liquidity (SSL) sweep, and an "Upthrust" is a Buy-Side Liquidity (BSL) sweep. Tick volume expansion during these events validates institutional intent, acting as a mechanical filter for the [Wyckoff-VSA-SMC Hybrid](concepts/wyckoff-vsa-smc-hybrid.md) model.

**7. AMD, Session Timing, and IOFED Execution (Relevance: 0.9 - 0.85)**
Algorithms operate on time. The [Power of 3 (AMD)](concepts/amd-power-of-3.md) dictates market rhythm: Asian session Accumulation, London session Manipulation (Judas Swing), and New York session Distribution. Advanced prop-firm traders mechanize this via the [Judas CHOCH](concepts/concepts/amd-power-of-3.md), trading the time-based sweep in Killzones combined with lower-timeframe displacement. For entry precision, the [IOFED (Institutional Order Flow Entry Drill)](concepts/iofed-precision-entry.md) dictates entering at the *proximal edge* of an FVG rather than waiting for 50% Equilibrium (EQ), sacrificing win rate for highly asymmetric Risk-to-Reward.

## Open Questions / Counter-Evidence

*   **Mechanical 'Judas CHOCH' vs. Higher Timeframe Alignment:** The heavily refined prop-firm [Judas CHOCH Strategy](concepts/concepts/amd-power-of-3.md) relies purely on time-based sweeps and 1-minute/3-minute structural shifts, actively ignoring Higher-Timeframe (HTF) directional bias if intra-session criteria are met. This explicitly contradicts the core SMC philosophy of top-down multi-timeframe alignment. Does isolating low-timeframe killzone sweeps create a sustainable, long-term edge, or does ignoring HTF narrative ultimately lead to structural degradation in volatile environments?
*   **IOFED Execution vs. FVG Equilibrium (EQ):** The [IOFED](concepts/iofed-precision-entry.md) technique demands entry at the proximal edge of an FVG to maximize Risk-to-Reward. However, algorithms frequently reprice to the 50% Equilibrium of an FVG or tap deeper into the originating Order Block. Is the higher R:R of proximal entries mathematically justified against the significantly higher rate of premature stop-outs? 
*   **Pure Price Action vs. Footprint Data Dependency:** The integration of DOM and footprint charts to validate [Order Flow Absorption](concepts/order-flow-absorption-dom.md) is powerful, but it risks dragging pure price action traders back into secondary data reliance (a form of indicator dependency). Does microscopic volume analysis genuinely filter out fake sweeps, or does it merely introduce low-timeframe noise that detracts from trusting clear structural displacement?
