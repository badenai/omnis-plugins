## Core Knowledge (by weight)

### 1. Market Structure & Structural Shifts
Market structure is the foundational lens through which price action is evaluated. It establishes the macroeconomic directional bias and filters out lower-timeframe noise [ict-smart-money-foundations](concepts/concepts/ict-smart-money-foundations.md).
*   **Structural Milestones:** Trends are objectively mapped using sequences of Higher Highs (HH), Higher Lows (HL), Lower Highs (LH), and Lower Lows (LL) [market-structure-and-imbalance-fundamentals](recent/2026-07/market-structure-and-imbalance-fundamentals.md).
*   **BOS vs. CHoCH:** 
    *   **Break of Structure (BOS):** Signifies trend continuation in alignment with the dominant order flow [smc-ict-gold-trading-framework](concepts/concepts/concepts/concepts/smc-ict-gold-trading-framework.md).
    *   **Change of Character (CHoCH):** Signals a potential trend reversal. A valid CHoCH demands an aggressive expansion (displacement) that closes a full candle body beyond a major, protected *external* swing high or low [structure-inducement-vs-choch](concepts/structure-inducement-vs-choch.md).
*   **Inducement (IDM):** Minor, internal structural breaks designed to trap premature breakout traders. Standard structural integrity (e.g., keeping higher lows intact during an uptrend) remains valid even when internal swings are swept for inducement, provided the major external structural level remains protected [market_liquidity_concepts](concepts/concepts/market_liquidity_concepts.md).
*   **Wicks vs. Bodies:** Wicks penetrating structural levels are classified as liquidity sweeps (stop hunts) rather than structural breaks, which require a decisive candle body close [structure-inducement-vs-choch](concepts/structure-inducement-vs-choch.md).

### 2. Market Liquidity Mechanics
Markets are viewed as highly algorithmic, non-random environments where price is systematically delivered to predictable liquidity pools to engineer transactions for large institutional participants [ict-killzones-and-imbalances](concepts/ict-killzones-and-imbalances.md).
*   **External vs. Internal Liquidity:**
    *   **External Liquidity:** Rests outside the active trading range at major swing highs (buy-side liquidity - BSL) and major swing lows (sell-side liquidity - SSL) [market_liquidity_concepts](concepts/concepts/market_liquidity_concepts.md).
    *   **Internal Liquidity:** Resides inside the current trading range at minor swing points, short-term pullbacks, and Fair Value Gaps. Price typically sweeps internal liquidity pools before seeking external liquidity targets [market_liquidity_concepts](concepts/concepts/market_liquidity_concepts.md).
*   **Equal Highs (EQH) & Equal Lows (EQL):** Double and triple tops/bottoms accumulate dense clusters of retail stop losses (buy stops above, sell stops below). As a level is tested repeatedly, retail traders view it as stronger support/resistance, systematically building deeper pools of high-volume institutional "fuel" [liquidity-sweeps-and-pools](concepts/concepts/liquidity-sweeps-and-pools.md).
*   **High vs. Low Resistance Liquidity:**
    *   **Low Resistance Liquidity (LRLR):** Forms after failure swings (where price fails to break a prior extreme). LRLR pools are weak and serve as highly magnetic draw-on-liquidity (DOL) targets [high-low-resistance-liquidity](concepts/concepts/high-low-resistance-liquidity.md).
    *   **High Resistance Liquidity (HRLR):** Forms after a clean structural sweep and immediate reversal (e.g., a higher high instantly followed by a lower low). Once HRLR is swept, price moves rapidly toward LRLR pools as they represent the path of least resistance [high-low-resistance-liquidity](concepts/concepts/high-low-resistance-liquidity.md).
*   **Contrarian Sentiment Alignment:** Retail sentiment serves as a powerful counter-indicator at extremes (e.g., >65% long/short). When retail positioning is heavily crowded, institutions sweep the corresponding stop-loss clusters to fuel reversals [liquidity-sweeps-and-sentiment-extremes](concepts/concepts/liquidity-sweeps-and-sentiment-extremes.md).

### 3. Institutional Pricing Arrays (PD Arrays)
Trading setups are categorized into Premium vs. Discount pricing arrays within a defined dealing range [ict-smart-money-foundations](concepts/concepts/ict-smart-money-foundations.md).
*   **Order Blocks (OB):** The structural footprint of institutional order execution, representing the last candle before a decisive structural market expansion or breakout [fvg-and-order-block-confluence](concepts/fvg-and-order-block-confluence.md).
*   **Breaker Blocks vs. Mitigation Blocks:**
    *   **Breaker Block:** A failed order block formed after price successfully sweeps liquidity (stop hunt of a previous extreme) and completes a Market Structure Shift (MSS). Breaker blocks pack significant institutional resting liquidity from swept stops, making them structurally robust [high-probability-order-blocks.md](concepts/concepts/high-probability-order-blocks.md).
    *   **Mitigation Block:** A failed order block formed by a failure swing (exhaustion) where price fails to sweep the previous extreme before breaking structural support/resistance. These represent trend fatigue, requiring tighter risk management and higher timeframe confluence [high-probability-order-blocks.md](concepts/concepts/high-probability-order-blocks.md).
*   **Hidden Order Blocks (HOB):** Institutional zones disguised within overlapping wicks of consecutive same-color candles on higher timeframes (HTF). Dropping down to a lower timeframe (LTF) inside the wick-overlap zone reveals a fully formed, unmitigated traditional order block [high-probability-order-blocks](concepts/concepts/concepts/high-probability-order-blocks.md).
*   **Fair Value Gaps (FVG):** Three-candle price imbalances where aggressive market moves leave an untraded price zone between the first and third candle wicks [ict-killzones-and-imbalances](concepts/ict-killzones-and-imbalances.md). Can exist as:
    *   *BISI:* Buyside Imbalance Sellside Inefficiency [ict-smart-money-foundations](concepts/concepts/ict-smart-money-foundations.md).
    *   *SIBI:* Sellside Imbalance Buyside Inefficiency [ict-smart-money-foundations](concepts/concepts/ict-smart-money-foundations.md).
    *   *Consequent Encroachment (CE):* The precise 50% midpoint of the FVG, acting as a key algorithmic level [ict-smart-money-foundations](concepts/concepts/ict-smart-money-foundations.md).
*   **Inverse Fair Value Gaps (IFVG):** Built on structural polarity, an IFVG occurs when price decisively closes past a standard FVG. Retests of these flipped zones offer highly mechanical, tight-risk entry parameters [smc-fvg-ob-mechanics](concepts/concepts/recent/2026-07/smc-fvg-ob-mechanics.md).
*   **Balanced Price Ranges (BPR):** Horizontal overlap of a bullish and bearish FVG in the same price band, representing complete algorithmic equilibrium and immediate rebalancing. High-probability execution involves entries at the BPR midpoint (CE) [balanced-price-range-bpr](concepts/balanced-price-range-bpr.md).
*   **Suspension Blocks:** Candlesticks hovering "suspended" between an upper and lower volume imbalance, where the candle body is completely overlapped by the wick of the prior candle. It serves as a tight, unmitigated algorithmic reloading zone [advanced-pd-arrays](concepts/concepts/concepts/advanced-pd-arrays.md).
*   **Optimal Trade Entry (OTE):** Location tool designed to quantify discount/premium boundaries. Defined by the 0.618 and 0.79 retracements of the most recent impulsive leg, with the 0.705 level serving as the algorithmic sweet spot [advanced-pd-arrays](concepts/concepts/concepts/advanced-pd-arrays.md).

### 4. Algorithmic Time & Price Theory
Price must never be analyzed in a vacuum; high-probability setups require alignment between key price levels (Premium/Discount) and specific session times [ict-market-structure-and-timing-mechanics](concepts/ict-market-structure-and-timing-mechanics.md).
*   **Power of Three (AMD):** The daily template of price delivery [ict-smart-money-foundations](concepts/concepts/ict-smart-money-foundations.md).
    1.  *Accumulation:* Low-volume Asian session consolidates price and builds resting liquidity above and below its range [amd-power-of-3](concepts/amd-power-of-3.md).
    2.  *Manipulation:* London session executes a false breakout (the Judas Swing) to sweep the accumulated Asian range liquidity and trap retail traders [amd-power-of-3](concepts/amd-power-of-3.md).
    3.  *Distribution:* New York session reverses off the London manipulation, expanding heavily toward the unmitigated side of the range [amd-power-of-3](concepts/amd-power-of-3.md).
*   **Kill Zones:** Specific high-probability daily trading windows of peak institutional volume and algorithmic activity [ict-killzones-and-fvgs](concepts/concepts/ict-killzones-and-fvgs.md).
    *   *London Open:* 2:00 AM – 5:00 AM ET [ict-killzones-and-imbalances](concepts/ict-killzones-and-imbalances.md).
    *   *New York Open / Silver Bullet:* 8:30 AM – 11:00 AM ET [ict-killzones-and-fvgs](concepts/concepts/ict-killzones-and-fvgs.md).
    *   *New York PM Session:* 1:30 PM – 4:00 PM ET [ict-killzones-and-fvgs](concepts/concepts/ict-killzones-and-fvgs.md).

### 5. Transactional Order Flow Integration
To remove discretionary errors, charting-based SMC concepts are validated using real-time software-based order flow dynamics [ict-smart-money-concepts](concepts/concepts/ict-smart-money-concepts.md).
*   **Footprint & Cumulative Volume Delta (CVD):** Reads executed market orders diagonally (Bid x Ask) to spot structural aggressive imbalances of 3x or higher [order-flow-absorption-dom.md](concepts/concepts/order-flow-absorption-dom.md).
*   **Absorption:** Confirmed at key structural levels (like HTF order blocks or volume profile High Volume Nodes) when massive volume prints on both bid and ask sides, yet price fails to advance. This indicates that institutional passive limit orders are soaking up aggressive market orders, verifying a reversal setup [order-flow-absorption-dom.md](concepts/concepts/order-flow-absorption-dom.md).
*   **Volume Profile Nodes:**
    *   *High Volume Nodes (HVNs):* Areas of price acceptance and consolidation [order-flow-absorption-dom](concepts/order-flow-absorption-dom.md).
    *   *Low Volume Nodes (LVNs):* High-speed voids where price accelerates due to lack of historical transactions [order-flow-absorption-dom](concepts/order-flow-absorption-dom.md).
    *   *Point of Control (POC):* The price level representing the absolute highest traded volume, acting as a major institutional anchor [order-flow-volume-profile](concepts/concepts/order-flow-volume-profile.md).

### 6. Mechanical Entry Models
*   **The Unicorn Model:** A high-precision mechanical setup defined as the direct horizontal overlap of an ICT Breaker Block and an ICT Fair Value Gap (FVG) [unicorn-model-entry.md](concepts/concepts/unicorn-model-entry.md).
    *   *A+ Criteria:* Session-based liquidity sweep followed by an aggressive displacement leg causing a Market Structure Shift (MSS), with the FVG nested directly inside the Breaker boundaries [unicorn-model-entry](concepts/concepts/concepts/unicorn-model-entry.md).
*   **The Venom Model:** Fuses the immediate rebalancing of a Balanced Price Range (BPR) with a preceding liquidity sweep [ict-venom-unicorn-models](concepts/unicorn-model-entry.md).
*   **IOFED (Institutional Order Flow Entry Drill):** High-momentum technique where entry is executed directly at the proximal edge of an FVG rather than waiting for a deep 50% consequent encroachment fill, yielding aggressive asymmetric risk-to-reward ratios [iofed-precision-entry](concepts/iofed-precision-entry.md).
*   **Smart Money Technique (SMT) Divergence:** Advanced institutional divergence occurring when one correlated asset sweeps a key high/low while a sister asset fails to do so (e.g., NQ sweeping a low while ES prints a higher low), signaling institutional accumulation and immediate reversal zones [smt-divergence-case-studies](recent/2026-07/smt-divergence-case-studies.md).

## Recent Developments (Last 30 Days)

### 1. Systematic and Automated Execution Tools
The automation of price action mapping and quantitative backtesting has experienced major advancements.
*   **Algorithmic SMC Libraries:** Open-source Python libraries have formalized discretionary SMC/ICT concepts (Order Blocks, FVGs, structural breaks, and OTE ranges) into programmatic rules for quantitative, unbiased backtesting [algorithmic-smart-money-concepts-python](concepts/concepts/algorithmic-smart-money-concepts-python.md).
*   **Pine Script v6 Integration:** TradingView's Pine Script v6 native footprint data allows traders to automate Point of Control (POC) and Value Area strategies, bridging the gap between discretionary charting and systematic execution [automated-volume-profile-execution](concepts/automated-volume-profile-execution.md).
*   **No-Repaint Visual Toolkits:** Commercial toolkits like AlgoatTV and Flux Charts have gained popularity by automating multi-timeframe supply/demand mapping and structural shift alerts (BOS, CHoCH) utilizing strict no-repaint, confirmed-bar-close logic [market-structure-and-indicators](recent/2026-07/market-structure-and-indicators.md).

### 2. Shift in Proprietary Trading Firm Risk & Evaluation Rules
Proprietary evaluation programs have restructured their rules, significantly altering execution parameters for funded traders.
*   **The Floating Risk Rule:** Static drawdown parameters are being replaced by dynamic risk models. Under this framework, algorithmic risk engines adjust daily loss limits, contract-sizing allowances, and maximum drawdown dynamically based on real-time market volatility and live account equity [prop-firm-mechanics-and-risk.md](concepts/concepts/prop-firm-mechanics-and-risk.md).
*   **Apex Rule Restructuring:** Apex Trader Funding implemented major changes, introducing End-of-Day (EOD) trailing drawdown accounts, reducing active trading requirements from eight to five days, and eliminating restrictive rules such as the 5:1 risk-to-reward ratio and the 30% Maximum Adverse Excursion (MAE) open position drawdown rule [risk-management-prop-firm-rules](concepts/concepts/ict-smc-framework.md).
*   **Tradeify 'Select Plan':** Tradeify introduced a Select evaluation plan featuring a 40% consistency rule, a 3-day minimum, and an end-of-day trailing drawdown, completely retiring their old intraday trailing drawdown model [prop-firm-mechanics](recent/2026-07/prop-firm-rules-tradeify.md).
*   **Lucid Trading Models:** Lucid introduced Flex evaluations with a 50% consistency rule, zero activation fees, and a path transitioning traders to live brokerage accounts after six successful payouts [prop-firm-mechanics](concepts/prop-firm-mechanics.md).

### 3. FVG Invalidation & Sweep Execution Protocols
*   **Distal Wick Stop Loss Rules:** For strategies like the Silver Bullet, placing stop-losses within the wick of the sweeping candle is heavily penalized; modern precision execution requires positioning stop-losses entirely beyond the extreme distal line of the sweep to survive local liquidity sweeps [ict-killzones-and-fvgs](concepts/concepts/ict-killzones-and-fvgs.md).

## Open Questions / Counter-Evidence

### 1. FVG Invalidation and Fill Rates
While retail trading narratives treat every Fair Value Gap (FVG) as a guaranteed reaction zone, quantitative data challenges this assumption.
*   **The 5,400-Gap Study:** A large-scale statistical backtest across major assets revealed that approximately 30% to 40% of printed Fair Value Gaps remain completely unfilled [smc-fvg-ob-mechanics](recent/2026-07/concepts/concepts/recent/2026-07/smc-fvg-ob-mechanics.md).
*   **Lower-Timeframe Traps:** Up to 84% of lower-timeframe (e.g., M15) FVGs that do get filled experience immediate structural failure if they are not backed by higher-timeframe points of interest (e.g., H4 Order Blocks or premium/discount arrays), proving that isolated FVG trading carries negative expectancy [smc-fvg-ob-mechanics](recent/2026-07/concepts/concepts/recent/2026-07/smc-fvg-ob-mechanics.md).

### 2. Limitations of Multimodal AI Chart Graders
The rapid rise of AI-powered chart screenshot graders and visual pattern-recognition tools has introduced notable operational drawbacks.
*   **Static Analysis Fallacy:** Multimodal AI systems can recognize and grade textbook geometric patterns (such as a bull flag or head-and-shoulders) on static screenshots, but they cannot predict how those patterns resolve because the next candle remains unprinted [market-structure-and-pattern-validation](recent/2026-07/market-structure-and-pattern-validation.md).
*   **Context Blindness:** Screenshot-based AI analysis has no access to live off-chart context—such as order book thickness, funding rates, open interest, on-chain flows, and real-time macroeconomic event releases—making visual-only AI grading highly unreliable for live execution [market-structure-vs-pattern-hype](recent/2026-07/concepts/concepts/recent/2026-07/smc-fvg-ob-mechanics.md).

### 3. Discrepancies in Backtest vs. Live Performance
Quant execution reports highlight a persistent performance gap in automated SMC strategies. 
*   **Backtest Overfitting:** Many rules-based Price Action systems look flawless on historical data but experience immediate performance drift and decay in live market environments. This is driven by curve-overfitting, lookahead bias, unmodeled exchange transaction costs, dirty data, and execution slippage during volatile session-open sweeps [fvg-and-order-block-confluence](concepts/fvg-and-order-block-confluence.md).
