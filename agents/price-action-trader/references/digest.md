## Core Knowledge (by weight)

### 1. Advanced Market Structure & Pricing Arrays
The foundation of professional price action relies on an objective, multi-timeframe structural mapping protocol that strips away the subjective noise of retail technical indicators.
*   **Structural Progression:** Trends are strictly mapped using Higher Highs (HH), Higher Lows (HL), Lower Highs (LH), and Lower Lows (LL) [market_structure_and-imbalance-fundamentals](recent/2026-07/market-structure-and-imbalance-fundamentals.md).
*   **BOS vs. CHOCH/MSS:** 
    *   **Break of Structure (BOS):** Confirms structural trend continuation. A valid BOS mathematically requires a **candle body close** beyond the previous swing high or low on the dominant execution timeframe [ict-macros-and-market-structure](recent/2026-07/ict-macros-and-market-structure.md). Wicks piercing a level without a body close are classified strictly as liquidity sweeps or stop-hunts [ict-market-structure-and-liquidity](concepts/ict-market-structure-and-liquidity.md).
    *   **Change of Character (CHOCH) / Market Structure Shift (MSS):** Signals early structural reversals. An MSS requires energetic displacement (strong, high-volume expansion candles) breaking a local swing high or low, demonstrating clear institutional order flow redirection [ict-macros-and-market-structure](recent/2026-07/ict-macros-and-market-structure.md).
*   **Premium vs. Discount Arrays:** Every trading range between a major swing high and swing low is divided into a dealing range using a Fibonacci framework:
    *   **Premium Zone:** Top 50% of the range (sell zones).
    *   **Equilibrium:** Exact 50% midpoint of the dealing range.
    *   **Discount Zone:** Bottom 50% of the range (buy zones).
    *   High-probability institutional setups strictly forbid opening longs in premium or shorts in discount zones, preventing execution in mid-range retail noise [ict-market-structure-and-liquidity](concepts/ict-market-structure-and-liquidity.md).
*   **Optimal Trade Entry (OTE):** A precise location tool designed to quantify discount/premium pricing. OTE zones are defined as the **61.8% (0.618) to 79% (0.79) retracements** of the most recent impulsive structural leg, with **70.5% (0.705)** acting as the algorithmic sweet spot [advanced-pd-arrays](concepts/concepts/concepts/advanced-pd-arrays.md). High-probability execution requires that the OTE zone directly overlaps with a pre-existing higher-timeframe Point of Interest (POI).

### 2. Algorithmic Price Imbalances & Institutional Footprints
Institutions do not trade using retail geometric patterns. Instead, their massive block orders leave distinct, traceable price delivery footprints on raw charts.
*   **Fair Value Gaps (FVG):** A three-candle price imbalance created by aggressive institutional displacement. It is defined as an untraded zone where the wicks of the first and third candles do not overlap [ict-kill-zones](concepts/ict-kill-zones.md).
    *   **Consequent Encroachment (CE):** The precise 50% midpoint of an FVG or a long candlestick wick shadow [ict-macros-and-market-structure](recent/2026-07/ict-macros-and-market-structure.md). CE acts as a highly sensitive dynamic support, resistance, or entry validation level [ict-macros-and-market-structure](recent/2026-07/ict-macros-and-market-structure.md).
    *   **Inverse Fair Value Gaps (IFVG):** Operates on structural polarity. When price closes decisively past the boundary of an existing FVG, the zone flips its structural role (a bullish FVG flips to a bearish IFVG acting as immediate resistance, and vice versa) [smc-fvg-ob-mechanics](concepts/concepts/recent/2026-07/smc-fvg-ob-mechanics.md).
*   **Balanced Price Ranges (BPR):** The ultimate horizontal equilibrium, established when a bullish FVG and a bearish FVG directly overlap in the same price band [balanced-price-range-bpr](concepts/concepts/balanced-price-range-bpr.md). BPRs signal immediate algorithmic rebalancing where one aggressive move is instantly neutralized by a counter-move, creating a highly reactive magnet where price has traded aggressively in both directions [balanced-price-range-bpr](concepts/concepts/balanced-price-range-bpr.md).
*   **Order Blocks (OB):** The last candle prior to a decisive, aggressive market breakout or expansion, marking the footprint where large institutional limit orders were filled [fvg-and-order-block-confluence](concepts/fvg-and-order-block-confluence.md).
*   **Breaker Blocks vs. Mitigation Blocks:**
    *   **Breaker Blocks (BB):** A failed order block formed after price successfully sweeps liquidity (stop-hunt of a previous extreme) and completes an MSS. Fueled by swept stop-losses, Breakers are structurally stronger [high-probability-order-blocks](concepts/concepts/high-probability-order-blocks.md).
    *   **Mitigation Blocks (MB):** A failed order block formed by a failure swing (exhaustion), where price fails to reach or sweep the previous extreme before reversing. They represent trend fatigue and require tighter risk parameters and higher-timeframe confluence [high-probability-order-blocks](concepts/concepts/high-probability-order-blocks.md).
*   **Unicorn Entry Model:** A high-precision entry setup defined as the horizontal overlap of an ICT Breaker Block and an FVG [unicorn-model-entry](concepts/concepts/unicorn-model-entry.md). It leverages dual-polarity pressure (a structural flip combined with an algorithmic imbalance) to force a sharp, low-drawdown reaction upon retest [unicorn-model-entry](concepts/concepts/unicorn-model-entry.md).
*   **Suspension Blocks:** A highly specialized candlestick sandwiched between an upper and a lower volume imbalance, where the body is completely overlapped by the wick of the prior candle [advanced-pd-arrays](concepts/concepts/concepts/advanced-pd-arrays.md). This lack of a standard wick-to-wick FVG masks the zone from basic SMC software, while the twin volume imbalances function as highly reliable institutional order-reloading zones [advanced-pd-arrays](concepts/concepts/concepts/advanced-pd-arrays.md).
*   **Hidden Order Blocks (HOB):** Institutional accumulation/distribution zones hidden within the overlapping wicks of consecutive same-color candles on a higher timeframe (HTF) [high-probability-order-blocks](concepts/concepts/concepts/high-probability-order-blocks.md). Dropping down to a lower timeframe (LTF) inside the wick-overlap zone reveals a fully formed, traditional order block [high-probability-order-blocks](concepts/concepts/concepts/high-probability-order-blocks.md).

### 3. Liquidity Pools, Sweeps, and Inducement
Price delivery algorithms are programmed to move from one liquidity pool to another, seeking the necessary counterparty volume to execute institutional orders.
*   **External vs. Internal Liquidity:**
    *   **External Range Liquidity (ERL):** Rests outside the current trading range at obvious major swing highs (Buy-side Liquidity - BSL) and major swing lows (Sell-side Liquidity - SSL) [market_liquidity_concepts](concepts/concepts/market_liquidity_concepts.md).
    *   **Internal Range Liquidity (IRL):** Sits inside the current range at minor swing points, short-term pullbacks, and FVGs [market_liquidity_concepts](concepts/concepts/market_liquidity_concepts.md).
*   **Liquidity Inducement (IDM):** A minor, counter-trend internal structural break designed to trap early retail breakout traders and sweep internal stop-losses [market_liquidity_concepts](concepts/concepts/market_liquidity_concepts.md). Price routinely sweeps IRL and IDM first to gather fuel before expanding toward major ERL targets [market_liquidity_concepts](concepts/concepts/market_liquidity_concepts.md).
*   **Stop-Loss Clusters & Structural Pools:** Dense clusters of retail stop losses accumulate at Equal Highs (EQH / double-tops) and Equal Lows (EQL / double-bottoms) [liquidity-sweeps-and-pools](concepts/concepts/liquidity-sweeps-and-pools.md). Every test of a horizontal support/resistance level or dynamic trendline increases the volume of the stop cluster, turning the level into an incredibly attractive magnet for institutional sweeps [liquidity-sweeps-and-pools](concepts/concepts/liquidity-sweeps-and-pools.md).
*   **High vs. Low Resistance Liquidity:**
    *   **Low Resistance Liquidity:** Forms after failure swings (the market tries to make a new high/low but fails). This forms weaker defense zones, serving as easy targets for price to draw toward [high-low-resistance-liquidity](concepts/concepts/high-low-resistance-liquidity.md).
    *   **High Resistance Liquidity:** Forms after a clean structural break and immediate reversal (e.g., a higher high followed immediately by a sharp breakdown). Once swept, price accelerates rapidly toward low resistance pools [high-low-resistance-liquidity](concepts/concepts/high-low-resistance-liquidity.md).

### 4. Time and Price Integration: Session Kill Zones & Algorithmic Macros
High-probability price action setups cannot be analyzed in a temporal vacuum. The algorithmic pricing engine operates on a strict, time-dependent delivery schedule.
*   **Intraday Session Kill Zones:** Specific daily time windows where volatility and institutional liquidity injection peak:
    *   **London Open Kill Zone:** 2:00 AM – 5:00 AM ET [ict-kill-zones](concepts/ict-kill-zones.md).
    *   **New York Open Kill Zone:** 8:30 AM – 11:00 AM ET [ict-kill-zones](concepts/ict-kill-zones.md).
    *   **New York PM Session:** 1:30 PM – 4:00 PM ET [ict-kill-zones](concepts/ict-kill-zones.md).
*   **The Power of Three (AMD Model):** The primary daily template mapping how the daily candlestick range is formulated:
    1.  **Accumulation (A):** Occurs during the low-volume Asian Session, building a horizontal range of resting buy/sell stops [ict-smc-framework](concepts/concepts/ict-smc-framework.md).
    2.  **Manipulation (M):** Usually executes during the London Open as a false run (the **Judas Swing**) in the opposite direction of the true daily expansion to hunt Asian range stops and trap retail breakout traders [ict-smc-framework](concepts/concepts/ict-smc-framework.md).
    3.  **Distribution (D):** Expands heavily during the New York sessions toward the actual daily target, clearing the unmitigated side of the range [ict-smc-framework](concepts/concepts/ict-smc-framework.md).
*   **Quarterly Theory (AMDX):** An evolution of the AMD model introducing a fourth phase (**X**), representing either late-session Continuation or Reversal [amd-power-of-3](concepts/concepts/amd-power-of-3.md). Dividing market rotations into rigid time-based quarters mathematically isolates the manipulation phase to protect open profits from sudden end-of-day algorithmic realignments [amd-power-of-3](concepts/concepts/amd-power-of-3.md).
*   **ICT Macros:** Precise 20-minute algorithmic windows inside each trading session where the price delivery algorithm is highly active, producing sudden sequences of sweeps, displacement, and rapid delivery [ict-macros-and-market-structure](recent/2026-07/ict-macros-and-market-structure.md).

### 5. Wyckoffian Auction Market Theory & Volume Microstructure
To confirm structural price action setups without lagging indicators, professional traders integrate classical Wyckoff schematics with real-time volume microstructure.
*   **Wyckoff Market Cycles:** Prices progress through institutional Accumulation (building positions), Markup (upward trend), Distribution (unloading positions), and Markdown (downward trend) [wyckoff-market-structure](concepts/wyckoff-market-structure.md).
    *   The SMC "Liquidity Sweep" is the modern structural equivalent of the Wyckoff "Spring" (in accumulation) or "Upthrust" (in distribution) [wyckoff-vsa-smc-hybrid](concepts/wyckoff-vsa-smc-hybrid.md).
*   **Volume Profile Arrays:**
    *   **Point of Control (POC):** The absolute price level with the highest traded volume in a range, acting as a major institutional anchor [order-flow-volume-profile](concepts/concepts/order-flow-volume-profile.md).
    *   **High Volume Nodes (HVN):** Price areas of acceptance where heavy volume was transacted, acting as magnets and strong support/resistance zones [order-flow-volume-profile](concepts/concepts/order-flow-volume-profile.md).
    *   **Low Volume Nodes (LVN):** Price voids representing lack of historical transactions where price accelerates rapidly due to order imbalances [order-flow-volume-profile](concepts/concepts/order-flow-volume-profile.md).
*   **Order Flow Microstructure (DOM & Footprint Charts):**
    *   **Diagonal Imbalances:** Tracking executed aggressive market orders (Bid x Ask) diagonally, requiring a 3x imbalance ratio to confirm institutional momentum [order-flow-absorption-dom](concepts/concepts/order-flow-absorption-dom.md).
    *   **Absorption:** Confirmed at key structural levels (such as HTF Order Blocks or VWAP) when massive market orders execute on both Bid and Ask footprint cells, yet price completely stalls [order-flow-absorption-dom](concepts/concepts/order-flow-absorption-dom.md). This confirms passive limit orders ("iceberg orders") are soaking up aggressive retail volume [order-flow-absorption-dom](concepts/concepts/order-flow-absorption-dom.md).
    *   **Cumulative Delta Divergence:** A structural divergence where price prints higher highs while Cumulative Volume Delta (CVD) registers a flat or negative slope, signaling that aggressive buyers are exhausting and passive sellers are absorbing the remaining order flow [order-flow-absorption-dom](concepts/concepts/order-flow-absorption-dom.md).

### 1. Hardened Silver Bullet Time-Based Execution Protocols
Modern execution protocols for the **ICT Silver Bullet** have shifted away from general price action toward a rigid, time-dependent macro-algorithmic process [ict-killzones-and-imbalances](concepts/concepts/ict-killzones-and-imbalances.md).
*   **Three One-Hour Windows:** Execution is strictly limited to London Open (3:00–4:00 AM EST), New York AM (10:00–11:00 AM EST), and New York PM (2:00–3:00 PM EST) [ict-killzones-and-imbalances](concepts/concepts/ict-killzones-and-imbalances.md). The New York AM hour remains mathematically proven as the single highest-probability macro window [ict-killzones-and-imbalances](concepts/concepts/ict-killzones-and-imbalances.md).

If displacement occurs without a preceding stop hunt, the setup is completely invalidated [ict-killzones-and-imbalances](concepts/concepts/ict-killzones-and-imbalances.md).
*   **Mathematical Entry Optimization:** Entries are optimized at the 50% Consequent Encroachment (CE) of the FVG, with invalidation placed strictly past the distal extreme of the sweep wick [ict-killzones-and-imbalances](concepts/concepts/ict-killzones-and-imbalances.md).

### 2. Empirical FVG Statistics & Mitigation of Backtest Performance Drift
A major quantitative shift in price action trading is the systematic filtration of Fair Value Gaps to reduce the "performance drift" between backtested strategies and live execution.
*   **The 5,400 FVG Study:** A statistical backtest spanning over two years across major crypto assets revealed that approximately **30% to 40% of printed FVGs remain unfilled** [smc-fvg-ob-mechanics](concepts/recent/2026-07/concepts/concepts/recent/2026-07/smc-fvg-ob-mechanics.md).
*   **The 84% Failure Trap:** Up to **84% of lower-timeframe (e.g., M15) FVGs that get filled immediately reverse and break structure** if they are not directly backed by a Higher Timeframe (HTF) Order Block or premium/discount array [smc-fvg-ob-mechanics](concepts/recent/2026-07/concepts/concepts/recent/2026-07/smc-fvg-ob-mechanics.md).
*   **Lookahead & Friction Mitigation:** Quantitative execution reports prove that live performance drift is caused by lookahead bias, dirty data, unmodeled execution costs (slippage/commissions), and parameter overfitting [smc-ict-fvg-confluence](recent/2026-07/smc-ict-fvg-confluence.md). To filter out noise, intraday FVGs must be graded by zone strength, requiring immediate institutional displacement (high volume delta) and a preceding liquidity sweep on the opposite side [smc-fvg-ob-mechanics](concepts/recent/2026-07/concepts/concepts/recent/2026-07/smc-fvg-ob-mechanics.md).

### 4. Dynamic Options GEX & Liquidity Density Regimes
Professional price action analysts are increasingly overlaying options-market microstructure to define market volatility regimes.
*   **Gamma Exposure (GEX) Regimes:** Positive GEX regimes act as volatility dampeners, transforming key options strikes into magnetic, mean-reverting support/resistance levels [gex-and-liquidity-density-regimes](concepts/gex-and-liquidity-density-regimes.md). Negative GEX regimes amplify market swings, accelerating directional momentum and breakout expansions [gex-and-liquidity-density-regimes](concepts/gex-and-liquidity-density-regimes.md).
*   **Zero Gamma Level (ZGL):** Serves as a critical institutional volatility trigger [gex-and-liquidity-density-regimes](concepts/gex-and-liquidity-density-regimes.md). Crossing below the ZGL flips dealers short gamma, rapidly accelerating market volatility and invalidating standard range-bound price action [gex-and-liquidity-density-regimes](concepts/gex-and-liquidity-density-regimes.md).
*   **Liquidity Density Index:** Calculated as Volume divided by Range, classifying price action into three institutional categories:
    1.  *Valid Moves:* High volume with expanding price range.
    2.  *Liquidity Vacuums:* Low volume on fast price runs, highly prone to immediate fakeouts and sweeps.
    3.  *Absorption:* High volume on compressed price wicks, indicating heavy institutional accumulation/distribution [gex-and-liquidity-density-regimes](concepts/gex-and-liquidity-density-regimes.md).

## Open Questions / Counter-Evidence

### 1. The Friction of Wick vs. Body Closures in Structural Breaks
A persistent point of friction exists between early reversal entry and conservative continuation confirmation.
*   **Reversal Models (MSS/CHOCH):** Modern implementations of the ICT 2022 model and specific reversal models maintain that a **wick break** of a swing high/low is structurally valid to confirm an MSS or Change of Character [ict-2022-model-execution](concepts/concepts/ict-2022-model-execution.md). 
*   **Continuation Models (BOS):** Strict programmatic filters and automated SMC libraries require a **candle body close** to validate a structural break [recent/2026-07/ict-macros-and-market-structure](recent/2026-07/ict-macros-and-market-structure.md).
*   **Operational Conflict:** Treating a wick break as an active MSS increases the frequency of entering "double-sweeps" or smart money traps, while waiting for a body close on low-timeframe charts frequently causes entry delay, drastically reducing the overall risk-to-reward ratio of the reversal trade.

### 2. Multi-Asset Correlation Deviations (DXY & NQ/ES SMT)
*   **DXY Divergence:** The structural gold (XAU/USD) playbook relies on tight negative correlations with the US Dollar Index (DXY) and Treasury Yields [smc-ict-gold-trading-framework](concepts/concepts/smc-ict-gold-trading-framework.md). However, during extreme macro events or geopolitical capital flights, both gold and the DXY frequently expand in tandem, invalidating standard correlation-based daily bias.
*   **SMT Divergence Faults:** The Smart Money Technique (SMT) divergence utilizes Nasdaq (NQ) and S&P 500 (ES) correlation at key swing lows to identify the stronger asset [smt-divergence-case-studies](recent/2026-07/smt-divergence-case-studies.md). Yet, during highly trending, bullish regimes, trading a bearish SMT divergence (fading NQ because ES swept its high while NQ failed to do so) often leads to immediate stop-outs as institutional order flow aggressively overrides the correlation divergence [market-structure-and-pd-arrays](concepts/concepts/concepts/concepts/advanced-pd-arrays.md).

### 3. The Limits of Screenshot-Based AI Chart Analyzers
With the commercialization of AI-powered trading copilots (such as Claude MCP TradingView integrations and automated screenshot graders), retail traders are increasingly outsourcing their technical analysis [ai-mcp-tradingview-integration](concepts/ai-mcp-tradingview-integration.md).
*   **Prediction vs. Grading:** While AI vision models can objectively grade current technical states, trend structures, and risk math, they are fundamentally non-predictive [market-reflexivity-and-setup-grading](concepts/concepts/market-reflexivity-and-setup-grading.md). Financial markets are adversarial, reflexive systems; any highly accurate predictive model would immediately alter the price structure it attempts to forecast, neutralizing itself [market-reflexivity-and-setup-grading](concepts/concepts/market-reflexivity-and-setup-grading.md).
*   **Contextual Blind Spots:** Screenshot-reading tools remain entirely blind to critical, off-chart context such as live order books, real-time depth of market (DOM), funding rates, options gamma levels, open interest, and macroeconomic calendars [market-reflexivity-and-setup-grading](concepts/concepts/market-reflexivity-and-setup-grading.md). Relying blindly on automated visual overlays without manual tape-reading skills turns a structural price action setup into another retail lagging trap.
