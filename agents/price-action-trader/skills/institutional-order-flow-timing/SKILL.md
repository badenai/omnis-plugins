## The Iron Law

```
Never map or execute an institutional order block or entry array unless it is anchored to a confirmed Higher Timeframe (HTF) liquidity sweep or structure, and executes strictly inside an algorithmic session window (London Open, NY AM, or NY PM).
```

## Behavioral Rules

1. **Verify Liquidity Sweep Prior to Entry:** You must confirm a clean liquidity sweep of a major session extreme, daily high/low, or equal highs/lows before hunting for a Market Structure Shift (MSS) or Change of Character (CHOCH).
2. **Differentiate CHOCH from Inducement (IDM):** You must classify structural breaks as Inducement (IDM) if price does not deliver a decisive body close past the swing high/low. Treat wick-only breaks or minor internal structural shifts as traps, not trend reversals.
3. **Validate Order Block Typology:**
   * **Breaker Blocks:** You must only map a Breaker Block if the block swept liquidity (stop hunt of a previous extreme) prior to the displacement that caused the MSS.
   * **Mitigation Blocks:** You must map a Mitigation Block only when price fails to sweep the previous extreme (exhaustion swing) before reversing.
   * **Hidden Order Blocks (HOB):** You must drill down to lower timeframes to locate the unmitigated candle body structure when identifying accumulation/distribution zones hidden within overlapping wicks of consecutive same-color HTF candles.
4. **Enforce Precision in Balanced Price Ranges (BPR):** You must prioritize BPR entry zones generated by displacement candles with a body-to-wick ratio exceeding 70% to capture high-momentum delivery.
5. **Execute Advanced Entry Models Mechanically:**
   * **The Unicorn Model:** You must require the horizontal overlap of an ICT Breaker Block and a Fair Value Gap (FVG) immediately following an MSS to execute.
   * **The Reaper Inversion FVG:** You must ensure the Inversion FVG sits in the discount portion of the impulsive leg for bullish setups, or the premium portion for bearish setups, specifically nested within the leg executing the initial sweep.
   * **The Suspension Block:** You must identify a candlestick body completely overlapped by the prior candle's wick, suspended between an upper and lower volume imbalance, to trade this precise algorithmic reload zone.
6. **Apply Time-of-Day Execution Filters:** You must restrict institutional order flow execution to the designated kill zones:
   * **London Open:** 2:00 AM – 5:00 AM ET.
   * **New York Open / Silver Bullet:** 8:30 AM – 11:00 AM ET.
   * **New York PM Session:** 1:30 PM – 4:00 PM ET.
7. **Analyze Footprint & DOM for Sweep Confirmation:** You must verify actual institutional absorption at key levels by confirming a surge in market orders (bid/ask volume imbalances > 3x) combined with passive limit order absorption (icebergs/stacking) on footprint and DOM tools.

## Red Flags

| Error | Retail Rationalization | Why It Is Wrong (Institutional Reality) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Trading Every FVG** | "Price is retracing to an imbalance, so I must enter on the touch." | Retail trap. Low-probability FVGs are run through; high-probability FVGs must be nested with HTF Order Blocks. |
| **Wick CHOCH** | "Price broke the level with a fast wick, the trend has reversed!" | This is Inducement (IDM) or a stop hunt designed to sweep early breakout traders before continuing the true HTF trend. |
| **Out-of-Session Execution** | "The structure is perfect on the 5-minute chart at 11:45 AM ET." | High-probability institutional algorithms are inactive or in rebalancing phases during lunch or dead zones; setups are prone to chop and failure. |
| **Fixed Size on Prop Accounts** | "I always trade 2% risk per setup regardless of market conditions." | Fails to account for prop firm "Floating Risk Rules" and changing volatility; contract-based, adaptive exposure is mandatory. |
| **Static Backtest Overfitting** | "My strategy backtests perfectly on historical static candle charts." | Ignores execution latency, ECN transaction friction, spread expansion during liquidity sweeps, and subjective grading of zones. |
