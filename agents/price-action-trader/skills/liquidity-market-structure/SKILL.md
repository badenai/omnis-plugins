## The Iron Law

```text
Never validate a Market Structure Shift (MSS) or assume a directional reversal unless it is immediately preceded by a clear sweep of a major liquidity pool (PDH, PDL, or Equal Highs/Lows) and accompanied by energetic price displacement.
```

## Behavioral Rules

*   **Define Higher Timeframe Bias:** Always establish directional bias by identifying External Range Liquidity targets on Higher Timeframes (Daily/4H) before analyzing lower-timeframe structure.
*   **Wait for the Purge:** Require price to hunt and sweep resting retail liquidity (Previous Day Highs/Lows, Equal Highs/Lows, session extremes) before anticipating any valid reversal.
*   **Demand Displacement:** Validate a Market Structure Shift (MSS) or Change of Character (CHOCH) only if the structural break is characterized by strong, impulsive, energetic price action.
*   **Filter Retail Noise:** Discard structural breaks formed by slow, overlapping, or low-volatility price action; treat them as continuous retail inducement rather than institutional shifts.
*   **Contextualize News Events:** Evaluate high-impact news events strictly as volatility catalysts designed to accelerate price toward predetermined liquidity pools; never predict the news outcome itself.
*   **Monitor for Successive Sweeps:** Require unmistakable displacement before confirming an initial MSS to protect against continuous expansion profiles (inducement traps) where price sweeps one side and immediately reverses to sweep the other.
*   **Track Price Delivery:** Always analyze price as moving from Internal Range Liquidity to External Range Liquidity, or vice versa, to maintain structural context.

## Red Flags

| Domain-Specific Rationalization | Why Wrong |
| :--- | :--- |
| "The trendline broke, so market structure has shifted." | Trendlines are engineered retail liquidity pools; wait for a horizontal structural level (HL/LH) to break with displacement after a liquidity sweep. |
| "Price barely broke the previous high, this is an MSS." | Overlapping or weak structural breaks are retail noise; a valid MSS strictly requires energetic institutional displacement. |
| "A reversal looks imminent on the 1m chart." | Lower-timeframe shifts executed without higher-timeframe liquidity context (moving from internal to external liquidity) are random noise. |
| "Trading the breakout of equal highs (EQH)." | Equal highs are buy-side liquidity magnets engineered to trap retail breakout traders; anticipate a sweep (stop hunt), never a breakout. |

## Quick Reference

| Concept | Actionable Directive |
| :--- | :--- |
| **PDH / PDL** | Mark Previous Day Highs and Lows; target these as the primary catalysts for institutional liquidity purges. |
| **EQH / EQL** | Identify Equal Highs/Lows as resting retail buy-side/sell-side liquidity; wait for price to sweep these levels before engaging. |
| **Liquidity Sweep** | Observe price wicking through a liquidity pool to trap retail traders; use this as the mandatory trigger for any reversal setup. |
| **MSS / CHOCH** | Wait for a Market Structure Shift or Change of Character *after* a sweep to confirm institutional directional intent. |
| **Displacement** | Mandate strong, impulsive candles when breaking structure; use this to filter out fakeouts and weak retail breaks. |
| **Internal vs External** | Map the structural narrative by tracking price as it delivers from internal liquidity (ranges/imbalances) to external liquidity (swing highs/lows). |
