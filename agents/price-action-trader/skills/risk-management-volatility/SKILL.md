## The Iron Law

```text
Define risk solely by market structure; mandate stop-loss placement immediately beyond the extreme wick of the liquidity sweep that initiated the setup, and permanently invalidate the trade if this structural level is breached.
```

## Behavioral Rules

*   Place stop-loss orders exclusively above the high or below the low of the specific wick that engineered the liquidity sweep (e.g., the PDH/PDL purge).
*   Abort the trade idea instantly and accept the loss if price action violates the structural sweep wick; never widen a stop-loss to accommodate a failing setup.
*   Suspend standard entry protocols during Tier-1 macroeconomic data releases (e.g., FOMC, CPI) to avoid getting caught in double-sided liquidity sweeps.
*   Wait for a clear, post-news Market Structure Shift (MSS) accompanied by high-timeframe displacement before committing capital following extreme volatility spikes.
*   Reduce position sizing or stand aside during prolonged, low-volatility conditions (e.g., summer ranges), as structural breaks during these periods often represent algorithmic chop rather than true institutional sponsorship.
*   Recalibrate your directional bias completely if a news event engineers double-sided sweeps (purging both buy-side and sell-side liquidity in rapid succession) until a definitive displacement leg materializes.

## Red Flags

| Rationalization | Why Wrong |
| :--- | :--- |
| "I will use a fixed 20-pip stop loss to guarantee a 1:3 risk-to-reward ratio." | Risk must be defined by objective structural invalidation points (sweep wicks), not arbitrary pip counts divorced from price action. |
| "Price swept my stop, but the MACD is still crossing, so I will widen my stop." | Breaching the protective sweep wick objectively invalidates the setup; lagging indicators cannot override compromised market structure. |
| "I am placing my entry right before CPI to catch the maximum expansion." | High-impact news frequently triggers double-sided liquidity hunts; entering beforehand exposes capital to unpredictable extreme volatility wicks. |
| "The 1-minute chart showed a CHOCH in this low-volume session, I'm going full size." | Low-volume environments often produce false displacement; trading them at full risk ignores the necessity of true institutional energy. |

## Quick Reference

| Condition / Concept | Required Action |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Stop-Loss Placement** | Place strictly beyond the extreme wick of the initiating liquidity sweep. |
| **Trade Invalidation** | Close position immediately if the protective sweep wick is breached. |
| **Low-Volume Chop** | Reduce risk or stand aside; demand extreme displacement to validate MSS. |
| **Tier-1 News (CPI/FOMC)** | Suspend entries until post-news volatility settles and structural bias is confirmed. |
| **Double-Sided Sweeps** | Wait for both sides to be purged, then trade the resulting structural displacement. |
