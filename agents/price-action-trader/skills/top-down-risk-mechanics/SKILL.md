## The Iron Law

```text
Never execute a lower-timeframe setup without explicit higher-timeframe directional alignment (Daily/4H), and never enter a trade without a predefined, binary stop-loss anchored to the exact wick that swept liquidity.
```

## Behavioral Rules

*   Establish directional bias exclusively on the Daily or 4-Hour charts before dropping to lower timeframes (15m, 5m, 1m).
*   Track price delivery logically from Internal Range Liquidity to External Range Liquidity across your timeframe matrix.
*   Ignore all 1m-15m low-timeframe structure shifts (CHOCH/MSS) if they contradict the established Daily/4H narrative.
*   Place stop-loss orders strictly above or below the extreme wick that purged liquidity to ensure structural protection by institutional sizing.
*   Treat all stop-loss invalidations as absolute and binary; if the extreme wick is breached, accept that the trade premise is objectively wrong and accept the loss.
*   Calculate risk-to-reward strictly based on the distance between the lower-timeframe structural stop and the higher-timeframe liquidity draw.
*   Size your position relative to the distance to the invalidation wick to ensure strict capital preservation on every setup.

## Red Flags

| Rationalization | Why it's wrong |
| :--- | :--- |
| "The 1-minute chart shows a strong CHOCH, I should execute immediately." | Lower-timeframe shifts without Daily/4-Hour alignment are usually retail inducement traps. |
| "I will widen my stop loss because upcoming high-impact news might cause a brief spike." | Invalidations are binary; widening stops abandons objective structural protection and destroys risk mechanics. |
| "I don't need to check the 4-Hour chart because I'm just scalping the 1-minute." | Scalping against the higher-timeframe draw on liquidity dramatically decreases probability and edge. |
| "I'll hold through this structural break; the macro Daily bias is still intact." | If the execution timeframe's sweep wick is breached, your specific entry model is invalid, regardless of macro bias. |
